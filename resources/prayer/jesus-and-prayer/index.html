Jesus and Prayer

Throughout the Gospels, Jesus demonstrated the importance of prayer. Before significant moments, during times of ministry, and in His greatest hour of suffering, He sought the Father in prayer. His example continues to encourage every believer to live a life of faithful communion with God.

The Perfect Example

Learning from Jesus

Jesus never treated prayer as an obligation or an occasional activity. Throughout His earthly ministry, He regularly withdrew to pray, thanked the Father, prayed before important decisions, interceded for His disciples, and remained faithful in prayer even during His suffering. The Gospels repeatedly portray Jesus making prayer an essential part of His life and ministry. :contentReference[oaicite:0]{index=0}

As you study these passages, don't simply notice that Jesus prayed. Notice when He prayed, why He prayed, and what His example teaches about trusting God in every season of life.

What can I learn about prayer by watching Jesus?

Moments of Prayer

When Jesus Prayed

The Gospels record many occasions when Jesus prayed. These moments remind us that prayer was woven throughout His earthly ministry. Whether He was preparing for an important decision, ministering to crowds, giving thanks, or facing suffering, Jesus continually sought the Father. As you read these passages, notice not only what Jesus prayed, but also the circumstances surrounding each prayer.

At His Baptism

Luke 3:21–22

As Jesus was baptized, He prayed, and the Holy Ghost descended upon Him while the Father's voice declared His pleasure.

Before Choosing the Twelve

Luke 6:12–13

Before selecting the twelve apostles, Jesus spent the night in prayer, demonstrating complete dependence upon the Father before making an important decision.

Giving Thanks

John 6:11

Before feeding the multitude, Jesus gave thanks, reminding us that gratitude should remain an important part of our own prayers.

Read John 6

At Lazarus' Tomb

John 11:41–42

Before calling Lazarus from the grave, Jesus prayed openly, giving thanks to the Father and strengthening the faith of those who were watching.

The High Priestly Prayer

John 17

Jesus prayed for Himself, His disciples, and all who would believe through their message. It is one of the longest recorded prayers of Jesus in Scripture.

In Gethsemane

Matthew 26:36–46

On the night before His crucifixion, Jesus prayed with complete submission to the Father's will, trusting Him even in the midst of deep sorrow.

Learning from Jesus

What His Example Teaches Us

Prayer Came First

Jesus often prayed before important moments in His ministry, reminding us to seek God's wisdom before making decisions.

He Prayed Regularly

Prayer was not reserved for emergencies. It was a consistent part of Jesus' daily life and relationship with the Father.

He Prayed with Thanksgiving

Jesus frequently expressed gratitude, showing us the importance of thanking God even before seeing the outcome.

He Prayed for Others

Jesus continually interceded for His disciples and those who would believe, demonstrating the importance of praying for others.

He Trusted God's Will

Even in Gethsemane, Jesus submitted Himself completely to the Father's will, providing the ultimate example of faith and obedience.

He Remained Faithful

From the beginning of His ministry to the cross, Jesus remained faithful in prayer, encouraging us to do the same throughout every season of life.

A Closer Look

Jesus' Prayer in John 17

Among all the prayers recorded in the Gospels, John 17 stands apart. Shortly before His arrest and crucifixion, Jesus prayed for Himself, for His disciples, and for all who would believe through their testimony. This chapter provides a remarkable glimpse into the heart of Jesus as He prepared to complete His earthly ministry.

As you read this chapter, notice how Jesus desires the Father to be glorified, prays for the protection and spiritual growth of His followers, and looks ahead to future generations of believers. His prayer reminds us that He cares deeply for those who belong to Him.

Following His Example

Growing Through Prayer

Jesus demonstrated that prayer was not merely something to do during times of difficulty. Prayer was woven into every part of His life and ministry. He prayed before important decisions, during times of service, in moments of thanksgiving, while interceding for others, and even in His deepest suffering.

As you continue growing in your own prayer life, remember that prayer is not about impressing God with many words. It is about faithfully drawing near to Him day after day. The example of Jesus encourages us to seek God with humility, trust His wisdom, and remain faithful in every season of life.

Watch and pray, that ye enter not into temptation: the spirit indeed is willing, but the flesh is weak.
Matthew 26:41

Notice What Jesus Prayed For

Themes Throughout His Prayers

As you read the Gospels, pay close attention to the focus of Jesus' prayers. They reveal His love for the Father, His compassion for people, and His complete commitment to God's will. These themes can also encourage and shape our own prayer lives.

The Father's Glory

Jesus continually sought to glorify the Father in everything He said and did. His prayers reflected a desire that God's name would be honored above all else.

God's Will

Even in moments of deep sorrow, Jesus trusted the Father's wisdom and willingly submitted Himself to His perfect will.

His Disciples

Jesus faithfully prayed for those who followed Him, asking that they would remain faithful, protected, and united.

Future Believers

Jesus also prayed for those who would believe through the testimony of His disciples, reminding us of His love for every generation of believers.

Thanksgiving

Whether before a miracle or while teaching others, Jesus regularly expressed gratitude to the Father.

Compassion

Jesus loved people deeply. His prayers reflected His compassion, mercy, and desire that people would know God.

Follow His Example

Grow Closer to God Through Prayer

Jesus showed us that prayer was not reserved for special occasions. It was woven into His daily life. He prayed in quiet places, before important decisions, during times of ministry, and in moments of suffering. His example reminds us that prayer is a continual relationship with God rather than an occasional activity.

As you continue reading the Gospels, look for the many moments when Jesus withdrew to pray. Notice His dependence upon the Father, His compassion for others, and His unwavering commitment to God's will. Allow His example to encourage your own walk with God each day.

Bible Reading Plans

Whether you're reading the Bible for the first time or beginning again, these reading plans are designed to help you stay rooted in Scripture one day at a time. Consistency matters more than speed. As you read, ask God to teach you, strengthen your faith, and help you apply His Word to your life.

Start Today

Growing Through God's Word

The Bible is God's inspired Word. Every page reveals His character, His promises, His plan of redemption, and His invitation to know Him more deeply. Reading Scripture regularly helps strengthen our faith, renew our minds, and guide our daily lives.

Don't worry about reading large portions each day. Begin with a simple plan, read carefully, pray before you begin, and allow God's Word to shape your heart over time.

A few faithful minutes each day can lead to a lifetime of spiritual growth.

Helpful Habits

Making Bible Reading a Daily Practice

Pray Before You Read

Ask God to open your understanding and help you apply His Word to your life.

Read Consistently

A few minutes every day often accomplishes more than occasional long reading sessions.

Take Notes

Write down verses that stand out, questions you have, and truths you want to remember.

Read Slowly

Don't rush to finish chapters. Take time to understand what the passage teaches about God.

Apply What You Read

Bible reading is meant to transform our lives, not simply increase our knowledge.

Keep Going

If you miss a day, simply begin again. Faithful consistency matters far more than perfection.

Stay in the Word

One Day at a Time

Every time you open the Bible, you are opening God's Word. You do not need to understand everything at once. Read prayerfully, ask questions, and allow Scripture to shape your heart one passage at a time. Spiritual growth comes through faithful consistency rather than hurried reading.

Wherever you begin, keep your eyes on Jesus Christ. As you continue reading, you will discover God's faithfulness, His promises, His plan of salvation, and His desire to transform your life through His Word.

Thy word is a lamp unto my feet, and a light unto my path.
Psalm 119:105

A Prayer Before Reading

Teach Me Through Your Word

Heavenly Father,

Thank You for giving us Your Word. As I read the Scriptures, help me to understand what You have revealed and to apply Your truth to my life. Give me a heart that is eager to learn, willing to obey, and faithful to continue growing in my relationship with You.

Open my eyes to see the beauty of Your Word and strengthen my faith through everything I read. May Your truth guide my thoughts, my decisions, and my walk with You each day.

In the name of Jesus Christ, Amen.

Receiving the Holy Ghost

Throughout the Bible, God promised to give His Spirit to His people. This study invites you to search the Scriptures and discover what the Bible teaches about receiving the Holy Ghost and His work in the lives of those who follow Jesus Christ.

Before You Begin

Open Your Bible

Before beginning this study, ask the Lord to give you understanding through His Word. Read every passage carefully in its context, compare Scripture with Scripture, and allow the Bible to shape your conclusions. The goal is not simply to gain information, but to know God more deeply and to follow Jesus Christ with a willing heart.

Theme

God's Promise Through Jesus Christ

The Holy Ghost is one of God's greatest promises to His people. Throughout the Scriptures we see this promise foretold, fulfilled, and experienced by believers. As you read these passages, observe who made the promise, how it was fulfilled, and what the Holy Ghost accomplishes in the lives of those who receive Him.

Then Peter said unto them, Repent, and be baptized every one of you in the name of Jesus Christ for the remission of sins, and ye shall receive the gift of the Holy Ghost.
Acts 2:38

Read the Scriptures

What Does the Bible Teach About the Holy Ghost?

Rather than depending upon tradition or personal opinion, take time to read each of the following passages carefully. Notice God's promises, the teaching of Jesus Christ, and the consistent pattern found throughout the Book of Acts. Ask the Lord to help you understand what His Word teaches about receiving the Holy Ghost.

Jesus Promised the Holy Ghost

"And I will pray the Father, and he shall give you another Comforter, that he may abide with you for ever."
John 14:16

Before His crucifixion, Jesus promised His disciples that they would not be left alone. Although He would soon ascend into heaven, He assured them that God would send the Comforter. This promise revealed God's desire to dwell with His people and to strengthen them for the work He had called them to do.

As you read this passage, notice that the promise of the Holy Ghost originated with Jesus Himself. Receiving the Holy Ghost was not an idea developed by the apostles after Pentecost. It was part of God's plan long before the church was established.

The Promise of the Father

"And, behold, I send the promise of my Father upon you: but tarry ye in the city of Jerusalem, until ye be endued with power from on high."
Luke 24:49

After His resurrection, Jesus again reminded His disciples of the Father's promise. Before beginning their ministry, they were instructed to wait in Jerusalem until they received power from on high. Their education, experiences, and time with Jesus were invaluable, but they still needed the promised gift of the Holy Ghost.

"But ye shall receive power, after that the Holy Ghost is come upon you: and ye shall be witnesses unto me both in Jerusalem, and in all Judaea, and in Samaria, and unto the uttermost part of the earth."
Acts 1:8

Jesus connected the coming of the Holy Ghost with power for witnessing. The disciples were not instructed to rely upon their own strength or abilities. God Himself would empower them through His Spirit so they could faithfully proclaim the gospel throughout the world.

The Day of Pentecost

"And they were all filled with the Holy Ghost, and began to speak with other tongues, as the Spirit gave them utterance."
Acts 2:4

On the Day of Pentecost, God's promise was fulfilled exactly as Jesus had said. The disciples were filled with the Holy Ghost while gathered together in Jerusalem. This event marked the beginning of the New Testament church and demonstrated that God's promise had become a present reality.

As you continue reading through Acts 2, notice how Peter explained what had happened. He pointed the crowd back to God's promises recorded in the Scriptures and declared that they were witnessing the fulfillment of what the prophets had spoken centuries before.

The Promise Was for All

"Now when they heard this, they were pricked in their heart, and said unto Peter and to the rest of the apostles, Men and brethren, what shall we do?"
Acts 2:37

After Peter preached Jesus Christ to the multitude, many were deeply convicted. They recognized their need for God and sincerely desired to know how they should respond. Their question remains one of the most important questions anyone can ask.

As you continue reading, notice that Peter did not answer with human opinion or religious tradition. His response was clear, direct, and centered upon God's plan of salvation.

"Then Peter said unto them, Repent, and be baptized every one of you in the name of Jesus Christ for the remission of sins, and ye shall receive the gift of the Holy Ghost."
Acts 2:38

Peter proclaimed that the gift of the Holy Ghost was part of God's promise to those who responded to the gospel. As you study this passage, consider why this was the apostle's first recorded answer after the death, burial, resurrection, and ascension of Jesus Christ.

"For the promise is unto you, and to your children, and to all that are afar off, even as many as the Lord our God shall call."
Acts 2:39

Notice the scope of God's promise. Peter declared that the promise of the Holy Ghost was not limited to those gathered in Jerusalem. It extended to future generations and to all whom the Lord would call. Consider what this reveals about God's desire to dwell with His people.

The Samaritans Received the Holy Ghost

"Then laid they their hands on them, and they received the Holy Ghost."
Acts 8:17

As the gospel spread beyond Jerusalem, the Samaritans also received the promise of the Holy Ghost. Observe how the same promise Jesus gave to His disciples continued as the gospel reached new people and new places.

The Gentiles Received the Holy Ghost

"While Peter yet spake these words, the Holy Ghost fell on all them which heard the word."
Acts 10:44

God's promise was not limited to the Jewish people. In the house of Cornelius, Gentiles also received the Holy Ghost, demonstrating that salvation through Jesus Christ was being offered to people from every nation. As you read Acts 10, notice how Peter recognized that God shows no partiality.

The Believers at Ephesus

"And when Paul had laid his hands upon them, the Holy Ghost came on them; and they spake with tongues, and prophesied."
Acts 19:6

Years after the Day of Pentecost, the Apostle Paul met disciples in Ephesus and asked them whether they had received the Holy Ghost. Their experience reminds us that God's promise continued throughout the ministry of the early church. As you read Acts 19, observe how consistently the apostles taught concerning the Holy Ghost and His work in the lives of believers.

Understand the Truth

Observe the Pattern in Scripture

As you compare these passages, notice the consistent pattern throughout the Book of Acts. Jesus promised the Holy Ghost before His ascension. The promise was fulfilled on the Day of Pentecost, and believers continued receiving the Holy Ghost as the gospel spread throughout the world. Rather than focusing on isolated verses, consider the testimony of the entire New Testament.

As you continue studying, ask yourself these questions. Who made the promise? To whom was the promise given? How did the apostles teach concerning the Holy Ghost? What do these passages reveal about God's desire to dwell with His people through His Spirit?

The purpose of this study is not to persuade you through human reasoning, but to encourage you to search the Scriptures carefully. Ask the Lord to give you understanding as you continue reading His Word and seek to follow Jesus Christ with a humble and obedient heart.

Live a Life That Pleases God

Receiving the Holy Ghost is not the end of the Christian life. It is the beginning of a daily walk with Jesus Christ. As believers grow in faith, they are called to live holy lives that reflect His character and bring glory to His name.

The New Testament continually encourages followers of Jesus Christ to walk in the Spirit, grow in grace, and faithfully serve the Lord until He returns.

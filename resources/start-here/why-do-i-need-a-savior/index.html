Why Do I Need a Savior?

Every person asks life's biggest questions. Why am I here? What happens after death? Can I be forgiven? The Bible answers every one of those questions by first revealing our greatest need. We need a Savior.

Before You Begin

Open Your Bible

Pray before you begin. Ask the Lord to help you read His Word with humility, honesty, and faith. Read each Scripture carefully, considering its context, and allow God to speak to your heart through His Word.

Theme

Every Person Needs Jesus Christ

The Bible teaches that every human being has sinned and stands in need of God's grace. Our greatest problem is not our circumstances, our past, or the opinions of others. Our greatest need is reconciliation with God, and that reconciliation is found only through Jesus Christ.

For all have sinned, and come short of the glory of God.
Romans 3:23

Read the Scriptures

Why Does Every Person Need a Savior?

The Bible answers this question with remarkable clarity. Before we can appreciate the good news of the gospel, we must first understand the condition of humanity. As you read the following passages, ask yourself what each one teaches about our relationship with God and our need for His grace.

God Created Us for Fellowship

So God created man in his own image, in the image of God created he him; male and female created he them.
Genesis 1:27

God created humanity with purpose. We were not created to live apart from Him but to know Him, walk with Him, and glorify Him. From the beginning, His desire was to have fellowship with those He created.

Sin Separates Us from God

For all have sinned, and come short of the glory of God.
Romans 3:23

Sin affects every person. It is not limited to a particular background, age, or nation. Scripture teaches that all have sinned, reminding us that every one of us stands in need of God's mercy.

Sin Has Consequences

For the wages of sin is death; but the gift of God is eternal life through Jesus Christ our Lord.
Romans 6:23

This verse presents two realities. Sin earns a wage that leads to death, yet God offers a gift that cannot be earned. Eternal life is given through Jesus Christ.

We Cannot Save Ourselves

Not by works of righteousness which we have done, but according to his mercy he saved us.
Titus 3:5

No amount of good works, personal effort, or religious activity can remove sin. Salvation is the result of God's mercy, not human achievement. This truth points us away from trusting ourselves and toward trusting the Savior.

God Made a Way

But God commendeth his love toward us, in that, while we were yet sinners, Christ died for us.
Romans 5:8

Even while humanity was separated from Him by sin, God demonstrated His love by providing the sacrifice we could never provide for ourselves. Jesus Christ came to accomplish what no one else could accomplish.

Understand the Truth

Our Greatest Need Meets God's Greatest Gift

Every person has different experiences, different struggles, and a different story. Yet the Bible reveals that we all share the same greatest need. We need forgiveness. We need reconciliation with God. We need the Savior He has provided through Jesus Christ.

The good news is that our story does not end with sin. God's love, mercy, and grace make a way for restoration through Jesus Christ. That is the message we will continue exploring in the next study.

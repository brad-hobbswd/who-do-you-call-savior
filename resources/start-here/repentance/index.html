Repentance

The gospel calls every person to respond. Throughout Scripture, repentance is presented as a sincere turning of the heart toward God. As you begin this study, ask the Lord to teach you what His Word says about repentance and what it means to follow Him faithfully.

Before You Begin

Open Your Bible

Pray before reading. Ask God to help you approach His Word with humility and a willingness to obey what He reveals. Read every passage carefully and allow the Scriptures to guide your understanding.

Theme

Turning Toward God

Repentance is more than recognizing that we have sinned. Throughout the Bible, it is connected with turning to God in sincerity, seeking His mercy, and desiring to walk in obedience to His Word. As you study the following passages, consider how Scripture describes repentance and what it reveals about God's invitation to every person.

Repent ye therefore, and be converted, that your sins may be blotted out.
Acts 3:19

Read the Scriptures

What Does the Bible Teach About Repentance?

Repentance is woven throughout the Scriptures. It was preached by the prophets, proclaimed by John the Baptist, emphasized by Jesus Christ, and preached by the apostles. As you read the following passages, notice how consistently God calls people to turn to Him with sincere hearts.

John the Baptist Prepared the Way

Repent ye: for the kingdom of heaven is at hand.
Matthew 3:2

Before Jesus began His earthly ministry, John the Baptist called people to repentance. His message prepared hearts to receive the coming Messiah and reminded people that God desired more than outward religion. He desired transformed hearts.

Jesus Christ Preached Repentance

The time is fulfilled, and the kingdom of God is at hand: repent ye, and believe the gospel.
Mark 1:15

The message of Jesus included repentance. His invitation was not merely to acknowledge His existence but to turn to God with faith and obedience. Repentance and belief are presented together as part of responding to the gospel.

Repentance Was Preached After the Resurrection

Repent, and be baptized every one of you in the name of Jesus Christ for the remission of sins, and ye shall receive the gift of the Holy Ghost.
Acts 2:38

On the Day of Pentecost, Peter answered the crowd's question by directing them to respond to the gospel. His words remind us that repentance occupies an important place in the biblical response to God's offer of salvation.

God Calls Everyone to Repent

And the times of this ignorance God winked at; but now commandeth all men every where to repent.
Acts 17:30

The call to repentance is universal. God's invitation extends to every person regardless of background, nationality, age, or past. His desire is that people turn to Him and receive the mercy He freely offers.

God Desires Repentance

The Lord is... not willing that any should perish, but that all should come to repentance.
2 Peter 3:9

Repentance reveals the heart of God. He delights in extending mercy and calling people to Himself. His invitation is not motivated by condemnation but by His desire that people know Him and experience the life He offers through Jesus Christ.

Understand the Truth

A Changed Direction Begins with a Changed Heart

As you have read these passages, notice that repentance is consistently connected with turning toward God. It is not simply feeling regret over sin. It is a sincere response to God's call that leads us to seek His mercy and submit our lives to His will.

Repentance prepares the heart to receive what God desires to do in our lives. It is not the end of the journey but one step in faithfully responding to the gospel of Jesus Christ.

Examine Your Heart

Reflect on God's Word

Take a few quiet moments before continuing. Ask the Lord to search your heart and allow His Word to reveal anything that needs to change. Biblical repentance begins with an honest heart before God.

Walk in Obedience

Repentance Leads to Faith

Throughout this study, we have seen God's loving invitation to turn toward Him. The next question naturally follows.

What is biblical faith?

Faith is more than believing certain facts. The Scriptures reveal a faith that trusts God, responds to His Word, and continues faithfully with Him.

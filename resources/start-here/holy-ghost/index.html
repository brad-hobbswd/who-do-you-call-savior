Receiving the Holy Ghost

Before Jesus ascended into heaven, He made an extraordinary promise to His disciples. He promised they would receive power from on high through the gift of the Holy Ghost. This study invites you to examine that promise through the Scriptures and follow its fulfillment throughout the Book of Acts.

Before You Begin

Open Your Bible

Ask the Lord to guide you as you read His Word. Read each passage carefully in its context and allow the Scriptures to explain the promise of the Holy Ghost. Keep your focus on what God has revealed rather than on personal opinions or traditions.

Theme

The Promise of the Father

Jesus promised that His followers would receive the Holy Ghost. That promise was fulfilled on the Day of Pentecost and continues throughout the Book of Acts. As you study these passages, notice who made the promise, how it was fulfilled, and what the Scriptures teach about this gift.

But ye shall receive power, after that the Holy Ghost is come upon you: and ye shall be witnesses unto me both in Jerusalem, and in all Judaea, and in Samaria, and unto the uttermost part of the earth.
Acts 1:8

Read the Scriptures

What Does the Bible Teach About the Holy Ghost?

Before His crucifixion and again before His ascension, Jesus promised His followers that they would receive the Holy Ghost. Rather than relying upon assumptions or traditions, let us carefully examine the Scriptures and observe how this promise unfolded.

Jesus Promised the Holy Ghost

And, behold, I send the promise of my Father upon you: but tarry ye in the city of Jerusalem, until ye be endued with power from on high.
Luke 24:49

Jesus instructed His disciples to wait in Jerusalem until they received the promise of the Father. This was not something they could accomplish through their own efforts. It was a gift God Himself would provide.

The Promise Was Fulfilled

And they were all filled with the Holy Ghost, and began to speak with other tongues, as the Spirit gave them utterance.
Acts 2:4

On the Day of Pentecost, the promise Jesus had spoken became a reality. The disciples were filled with the Holy Ghost, and Luke carefully records what took place. As you read Acts chapter 2, notice how Peter explained these events to the crowd.

The Promise Was for Others Also

For the promise is unto you, and to your children, and to all that are afar off, even as many as the Lord our God shall call.
Acts 2:39

Peter declared that God's promise extended beyond those gathered in Jerusalem. Consider who Peter says the promise is for and what that reveals about God's desire to pour out His Spirit.

The Samaritans Received the Holy Ghost

Then laid they their hands on them, and they received the Holy Ghost.
Acts 8:17

As the gospel spread into Samaria, believers there also received the Holy Ghost. This demonstrated that God's promise was not limited to one city or one group of people but accompanied the spread of the gospel.

The Gentiles Received the Holy Ghost

While Peter yet spake these words, the Holy Ghost fell on all them which heard the word.
Acts 10:44

The household of Cornelius became another powerful testimony that God's promise extended to the Gentiles. Peter later reflected upon this event as evidence that God shows no partiality in offering salvation.

Believers at Ephesus

And when Paul had laid his hands upon them, the Holy Ghost came on them; and they spake with tongues, and prophesied.
Acts 19:6

Years after the Day of Pentecost, believers in Ephesus also received the Holy Ghost. As you compare Acts 2, Acts 8, Acts 10, and Acts 19, observe the continuity of God's promise throughout the Book of Acts.

Understand the Truth

God Keeps His Promises

From Jerusalem to Samaria, from the household of Cornelius to the believers at Ephesus, the Book of Acts consistently records God's faithfulness to His promise. Jesus said His followers would receive the Holy Ghost, and the Scriptures show that He fulfilled exactly what He promised.

As you continue studying these passages, ask yourself what they reveal about God's faithfulness, His desire to dwell within His people, and the work of the Holy Ghost in the life of every believer.

Living for Christ

Receiving God's promises is not the end of the journey. Throughout the New Testament, believers were encouraged to continue growing in their relationship with Jesus Christ, allowing His Word to shape every part of their lives.

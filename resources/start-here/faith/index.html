Faith

Faith is one of the most familiar words in the Bible, yet it is often misunderstood. The Scriptures reveal that biblical faith is far more than believing that God exists. It is trusting Him, believing His promises, and responding to His Word with a willing heart.

Before You Begin

Open Your Bible

Begin this study with prayer. Ask the Lord to help you understand what genuine faith looks like according to the Scriptures. Read each passage carefully, keeping your Bible open, and allow God's Word to strengthen your trust in Him.

Theme

Trusting God and His Word

Biblical faith is confidence in God's character, His promises, and His Word. It is not based on feelings or circumstances but on the unchanging faithfulness of God. As you study these passages, consider what the Bible teaches about trusting Him in every area of life.

Now faith is the substance of things hoped for, the evidence of things not seen.
Hebrews 11:1

Read the Scriptures

What Does the Bible Teach About Faith?

Faith is found from Genesis to Revelation. Throughout the Scriptures, men and women placed their confidence in God, believed His promises, and obeyed His Word. As you read these passages, notice that biblical faith is not passive. It responds to what God has spoken.

Faith Believes God's Word

So then faith cometh by hearing, and hearing by the word of God.
Romans 10:17

Biblical faith begins with God's Word. The more we hear, read, study, and obey the Scriptures, the stronger our faith becomes. Faith is not built upon opinions or emotions but upon the truth God has revealed.

Faith Pleases God

But without faith it is impossible to please him.
Hebrews 11:6

Faith is essential to our relationship with God. Those who seek Him must believe that He is faithful and that He rewards those who diligently seek Him. Faith places its confidence in God's character even when circumstances are uncertain.

Faith Produces Obedience

By faith Noah, being warned of God of things not seen as yet, moved with fear, prepared an ark to the saving of his house.
Hebrews 11:7

Noah's faith was demonstrated through obedience. He trusted God's warning long before the flood arrived and acted upon what God had spoken. His life reminds us that genuine faith responds with obedience.

Faith Trusts God's Promises

By faith Abraham, when he was called to go out into a place which he should after receive for an inheritance, obeyed.
Hebrews 11:8

Abraham did not know every detail of the journey ahead, yet he trusted the One who called him. Faith often moves forward before every question has been answered because it rests in God's faithfulness rather than human understanding.

Faith Reaches for Jesus

Daughter, be of good comfort: thy faith hath made thee whole.
Matthew 9:22

The woman who had suffered for many years came to Jesus believing that He alone could help her. Her faith was not merely an idea. It moved her to seek the Savior with confidence that He was able to meet her need.

Faith Looks to Jesus

Looking unto Jesus the author and finisher of our faith.
Hebrews 12:2

The Christian life begins and continues by looking to Jesus Christ. Our confidence is not in ourselves but in the One who is faithful. As we keep our eyes on Him, our faith grows stronger through His Word and His presence.

Understand the Truth

Faith Trusts God Enough to Obey Him

Throughout this study, we have seen that biblical faith is more than agreeing that God exists. It is believing what He has spoken and responding with willing obedience. Whether it was Noah building the ark, Abraham leaving his homeland, or the woman reaching for Jesus, genuine faith always looked beyond circumstances and trusted God.

As your faith grows through the Scriptures, ask the Lord to help you trust Him more deeply each day. The same God who was faithful to His people throughout the Bible remains faithful today.

Examine Your Heart

Reflect on Your Faith

Take a few moments to think about what you have read in God's Word. Faith grows as we hear the Scriptures, trust God's promises, and respond to Him with obedience. Ask the Lord to strengthen your faith as you continue this journey.

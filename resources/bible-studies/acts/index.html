The Book of Acts

Discovering the Birth of the New Testament Church

Welcome

The Gospel Continues

The Book of Acts records the continuing work of Jesus Christ after His resurrection and ascension. Through the power of the Holy Ghost, the apostles proclaimed the gospel, established churches, and witnessed the message of salvation spread from Jerusalem to the ends of the earth.

Acts is not merely a history book. It is a testimony of God's faithfulness, the power of the Holy Ghost, the courage of ordinary believers, and the unstoppable advance of the gospel.

As you study this book, notice how prayer, obedience, faith, and the work of the Holy Ghost shape every chapter.

Ask yourself one question throughout this study.

How did the early church respond to the risen Jesus Christ?

Allow God's Word to answer that question as you journey through the Book of Acts.

About This Book

Getting to Know the Book of Acts

The Book of Acts bridges the Gospels and the New Testament letters. It records the birth of the New Testament church, the coming of the Holy Ghost, the ministry of the apostles, and the spread of the gospel throughout the Roman world. As you read Acts, notice how God continues working through ordinary men and women who faithfully obey Him.

Acts reminds us that the risen Jesus Christ is still building His church. Through the power of the Holy Ghost, believers proclaimed the gospel, endured persecution, served one another, and witnessed lives transformed by God's grace.

Traditional Author

Luke, the beloved physician and companion of the Apostle Paul.

Testament

New Testament

Literary Genre

Historical Narrative

Chapters

28 Chapters

Historical Setting

From Jesus' ascension through Paul's imprisonment in Rome, tracing the spread of the gospel across the Roman Empire.

Central Theme

The continued work of Jesus Christ through the Holy Ghost and His church.

Purpose of Acts

Why This Book Was Written

The Book of Acts shows how the gospel spread from Jerusalem to Judea, Samaria, and eventually throughout the known world. It records how believers depended upon God through prayer, were empowered by the Holy Ghost, proclaimed Jesus Christ boldly, and established churches wherever the gospel was received.

As you study Acts, look for the repeated themes of prayer, obedience, boldness, fellowship, generosity, perseverance, and faithful witness. These qualities appear throughout the book and continue to encourage believers today.

But ye shall receive power, after that the Holy Ghost is come upon you: and ye shall be witnesses unto me both in Jerusalem, and in all Judaea, and in Samaria, and unto the uttermost part of the earth.
Acts 1:8

This verse serves as a roadmap for the Book of Acts. The gospel begins in Jerusalem, spreads throughout Judea and Samaria, and ultimately reaches the nations, demonstrating God's desire for people everywhere to hear the good news of Jesus Christ.

Journey Through Acts

The Story of the Early Church

The Book of Acts tells one continuous story. Beginning with the ascension of Jesus Christ, it follows the birth and growth of the New Testament church as the gospel spreads from Jerusalem to the nations. These major events provide a helpful overview before reading the book for yourself.

The Ascension

Acts 1

After His resurrection, Jesus commissions His disciples before ascending into heaven, promising that they will receive power through the Holy Ghost.

Read Acts 1

The Day of Pentecost

Acts 2

The Holy Ghost is poured out upon the believers, Peter preaches the gospel, and thousands respond to the message of Jesus Christ.

Read Acts 2

The Church Grows

Acts 2–5

Believers devote themselves to the apostles' teaching, fellowship, prayer, and worship while God continues adding to the church.

Read Acts 2

Stephen's Witness

Acts 6–7

Stephen boldly proclaims the truth about Jesus Christ and becomes the first Christian martyr recorded in Scripture.

Read Acts 7

Philip Shares the Gospel

Acts 8

Philip faithfully preaches Christ in Samaria and later explains the Scriptures to the Ethiopian official, who believes and is baptized.

Read Acts 8

Saul Meets Jesus

Acts 9

On the road to Damascus, Saul encounters the risen Lord. His life is transformed, and he begins the journey that will make him one of the greatest missionaries in history.

Read Acts 9

The Gospel Reaches the Gentiles

Acts 10

Peter visits Cornelius, demonstrating that the gospel is proclaimed beyond Israel as God gathers people from every nation.

Read Acts 10

Missionary Journeys

Acts 13–21

Paul and his companions travel throughout the Roman world preaching Jesus Christ, strengthening believers, and establishing churches.

Read Acts 13

The Gospel Reaches Rome

Acts 28

Although imprisoned, Paul continues proclaiming the kingdom of God with confidence, showing that the gospel cannot be stopped.

Read Acts 28

Major Themes

What You'll Discover Throughout Acts

As you read the Book of Acts, you'll notice several themes appearing again and again. These themes help us understand not only the early church but also encourage us in our own walk with Jesus Christ today.

🔥 The Holy Ghost

From Pentecost onward, the Holy Ghost empowers believers to proclaim the gospel, strengthens the church, provides guidance, and equips God's people for faithful service.

🙏 Prayer

Before major decisions, during persecution, and in times of need, believers continually seek God through prayer, demonstrating complete dependence upon Him.

📖 The Gospel

The good news of Jesus Christ remains the central message of Acts. Whether speaking to crowds, individuals, rulers, or communities, the apostles faithfully proclaim the gospel.

🤝 Fellowship

The early believers devoted themselves to worship, teaching, prayer, generosity, and caring for one another, providing a beautiful picture of Christian community.

❤️ Generosity

Acts demonstrates believers willingly sharing with those in need, reflecting God's love through practical acts of kindness and compassion.

💪 Courage

Despite opposition, imprisonment, and persecution, the apostles continue proclaiming Jesus Christ with confidence and faith.

🌍 Missions

The gospel spreads from Jerusalem throughout Judea, Samaria, and eventually into the Roman world, fulfilling the mission Jesus gave His disciples.

⛪ The Church

Acts records the birth and growth of the New Testament church as believers gather together for worship, teaching, fellowship, prayer, and service.

👑 Jesus Christ

Although Jesus ascends into heaven in the opening chapter, His presence and authority remain central throughout the entire book as His followers faithfully proclaim His name.

Important People

Men and Women God Used

Acts introduces many faithful servants whose lives remind us that God works through people who are willing to trust and obey Him.

Peter

A bold apostle whose preaching at Pentecost helped establish the early church.

John

A faithful witness to Jesus Christ who ministered alongside Peter during the early years of the church.

Stephen

Known for his wisdom, faith, and unwavering testimony even in the face of death.

Philip

An evangelist whose willingness to follow God's leading brought the gospel to many people.

Barnabas

Often called the Son of Encouragement, Barnabas strengthened believers and supported Paul's ministry.

Paul

Once a persecutor of the church, Paul became one of Christianity's most influential missionaries after encountering the risen Jesus Christ.

Silas

A faithful companion who continued praising God even while imprisoned.

Timothy

A young believer who faithfully served alongside Paul and grew into a trusted leader.

Reading Guide

How to Study the Book of Acts

The Book of Acts is best understood when read as one continuous story. Rather than reading isolated verses, work through the book carefully and observe how God works through ordinary people who faithfully follow Jesus Christ.

Pray Before Reading

Ask God for wisdom and understanding as you open His Word.

Read One Chapter Daily

Take your time and allow each chapter to build upon the previous one.

Notice the Holy Ghost

Observe how the Holy Ghost guides, strengthens, comforts, and empowers believers throughout Acts.

Watch the Gospel Spread

Notice how the message of Jesus Christ moves from Jerusalem to many different cities and regions.

Write Your Observations

Keep a notebook of passages, questions, answered prayers, and lessons you discover while reading.

Apply God's Word

Ask how the examples of faith, prayer, courage, and obedience encourage your own walk with Jesus Christ today.

Looking for Jesus

Jesus Christ Throughout the Book of Acts

Although Jesus ascends into heaven in the opening chapter of Acts, His presence is felt throughout the entire book. The apostles preach His name, believers trust His promises, the Holy Ghost empowers His followers, and lives continue to be transformed through the gospel.

As you read Acts, notice how often Jesus Christ remains at the center of every sermon, every miracle, every decision, and every act of faithful obedience. The story is not simply about the apostles. It is about the continuing work of the risen Savior through His people.

Jesus Christ the same yesterday, and to day, and for ever.
Hebrews 13:8

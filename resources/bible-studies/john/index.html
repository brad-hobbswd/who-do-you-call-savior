The Gospel of John

Discovering Jesus Christ Through the Gospel of John

Welcome

Come and See Jesus

If someone asked where to begin reading the Bible, the Gospel of John would be one of my first recommendations.

John introduces us to Jesus Christ in a deeply personal way. Throughout this Gospel we witness His compassion, His authority, His miracles, His teachings, His death, His resurrection, and His invitation for every person to believe in Him.

This study is not intended to replace reading Scripture. Rather, it is designed to encourage you to open your Bible, read the Gospel carefully, and discover for yourself what John reveals about Jesus Christ.

As you work through this study, return often to one simple question.

Who does John reveal Jesus Christ to be?

Allow God's Word to answer that question, and let the Holy Ghost guide your understanding as you seek Him through the Scriptures.

About This Book

Getting to Know the Gospel of John

The Gospel of John is the fourth book of the New Testament and one of the four Gospel accounts that record the life and ministry of Jesus Christ. While each Gospel presents a unique perspective, John carefully selects events and teachings that encourage readers to believe in Jesus Christ and find life through His name.

Rather than attempting to record every event of Jesus' earthly ministry, John focuses on carefully chosen signs, conversations, and teachings that reveal the identity of Jesus Christ. As you study this Gospel, take your time, read each passage within its context, and allow Scripture to interpret Scripture.

Traditional Author

John, one of the twelve apostles and a member of Jesus' inner circle alongside Peter and James.

Testament

New Testament

Literary Genre

Gospel

Chapters

21 Chapters

Central Purpose

To lead readers to believe that Jesus is the Christ and to find life through His name.

Key Verse

John 20:30–31

Why John Wrote This Gospel

The Purpose of the Book

Unlike many ancient writings, the Gospel of John clearly tells us why it was written. Near the conclusion of the book, John explains that he selected particular signs and testimonies so that readers might believe in Jesus Christ.

And many other signs truly did Jesus in the presence of his disciples, which are not written in this book: But these are written, that ye might believe that Jesus is the Christ, the Son of God; and that believing ye might have life through his name.
John 20:30–31

As you continue reading, keep this purpose in mind. Every miracle, every conversation, every teaching, and every sign recorded by John contributes to his overall message. Ask yourself how each passage helps you better understand Jesus Christ and strengthens your faith in Him.

Keep Notes

Look for Jesus

As you read, continually ask yourself, "What does this passage reveal about Jesus Christ?"

The Seven Signs

Miracles That Reveal Jesus Christ

John refers to these miracles as signs because they point beyond the miracle itself. Each one reveals something about the identity, authority, compassion, and glory of Jesus Christ. As you read these passages, ask yourself not only what happened, but what John wants you to understand about the Savior.

The Great "I Am" Statements

How Jesus Reveals Himself

Throughout the Gospel of John, Jesus uses a series of memorable "I am" statements to teach His disciples and those around Him. Each declaration reveals something about His character, His mission, and the relationship He desires to have with those who follow Him. As you read these passages, consider what each statement teaches about Jesus Christ and what it means for your own life.

"I Am the Bread of Life"

John 6:35

Jesus alone satisfies the deepest hunger of the human heart and offers eternal life to those who come to Him in faith.

Read John 8

"I Am the Door"

Names and Titles of Jesus

How John Describes the Savior

Throughout the Gospel, Jesus is identified by many names and titles. Each one reveals something about His person, His mission, and His relationship with those who believe in Him. As you read John's Gospel, pay attention to these descriptions and consider what each teaches about Jesus Christ.

The Word

John 1:1, 14

John begins his Gospel by introducing Jesus as the eternal Word, revealing God's self disclosure to humanity.

Read John 1

The Lamb of God

John 1:29

Rabbi

Jesus is recognized as a Teacher whose words lead people toward the truth.

Messiah

Son of God

Reading Guide

How to Study the Gospel of John

Begin with Prayer

Ask God to give you wisdom, understanding, and a heart that is willing to receive His Word.

Read the Whole Chapter

Rather than focusing only on individual verses, read each chapter in its entirety to better understand the context.

Observe Carefully

Notice what Jesus says, what He does, how people respond to Him, and what John emphasizes throughout the Gospel.

Compare Scripture

Allow the Bible to explain itself by comparing related passages throughout Scripture.

Write Your Observations

Keep a journal of verses, questions, and truths that stand out as you read.

Apply God's Word

Ask how each passage encourages you to trust Jesus Christ more completely and live according to His Word.

The Gospel of John

The Gospel of John invites every reader to discover who Jesus Christ truly is. Through His words, miracles, signs, and resurrection, John presents Jesus as the promised Messiah and calls every person to believe in Him and receive eternal life.

An Invitation

Come and See Jesus

If you are reading the Bible for the first time, there is perhaps no better place to begin than the Gospel of John.

John does more than record historical events. He introduces us to Jesus Christ. Every conversation, miracle, and teaching reveals His glory and invites us to believe that He is the Christ, the Son of God, and that believing we might have life through His name.

As you study this Gospel, ask one question repeatedly:

What does this chapter reveal about Jesus Christ?

Allow the Scriptures to answer that question, and let God's Word shape your understanding of the Savior.

Meet Jesus

The Gospel According to John

John's Gospel is unlike the other three Gospels. While Matthew emphasizes Jesus as the promised King, Mark presents Him as the faithful Servant, and Luke highlights His humanity, John draws our attention to the identity of Jesus Christ and invites us to believe in Him.

Throughout this Gospel, Jesus speaks, teaches, performs miracles, forgives sins, reveals His glory, and demonstrates the love of God. Every chapter encourages us to look beyond the events themselves and consider who Jesus truly is.

John tells us exactly why he wrote this book.

But these are written, that ye might believe that Jesus is the Christ, the Son of God; and that believing ye might have life through his name.
John 20:31

As you read through John's Gospel, keep asking yourself one simple question:

What is John revealing about Jesus Christ?

Discover Jesus

Major Themes in John's Gospel

The Eternal Word

John opens by declaring that the Word existed in the beginning, was with God, and was God, before revealing that the Word became flesh and dwelt among us.

The Lamb of God

John the Baptist points to Jesus as the Lamb of God who takes away the sin of the world.

The Great "I Am" Statements

Jesus reveals Himself through powerful declarations such as the Bread of Life, the Light of the World, the Good Shepherd, the Resurrection and the Life, the True Vine, and more.

Signs and Miracles

John records selected signs that reveal the glory, authority, compassion, and power of Jesus Christ.

Believing in Jesus

Faith is one of the central themes of John's Gospel as readers are continually invited to believe in Jesus Christ and receive eternal life.

Life Through His Name

John concludes by reminding readers that his purpose is to lead people to believe in Jesus Christ and find life through His name.

Read the Gospel

Study One Chapter at a Time

The Gospel of John contains twenty-one chapters. Rather than rushing through them, consider reading one chapter each day. Pray before you begin, read carefully, and take time to reflect on what each chapter teaches about Jesus Christ.

John's Purpose

Believe in Jesus Christ

John never loses sight of his purpose. Every miracle, every conversation, every sign, and every discourse points the reader toward one question.

Who is Jesus Christ?

As you continue reading, allow the Gospel itself to answer that question. Observe what Jesus says, what He does, and how those around Him respond. Ask the Lord to help you understand His Word and to strengthen your faith through the testimony of Scripture.

He that hath seen me hath seen the Father.
John 14:9

Study the Scriptures

Every Book Tells Part of God's Story

Although the Bible contains sixty six books written by many authors over many centuries, it tells one unified story. From the opening chapters of Genesis to the closing words of Revelation, God's plan of redemption unfolds through His promises, His faithfulness, and ultimately through Jesus Christ.

These studies are designed to help you understand each book within its historical setting while seeing how it contributes to the greater story of Scripture.

Take your time. Read each book prayerfully, study it carefully, and allow God's Word to shape your heart and strengthen your faith.

The Bible Is One Story

Every Book Points to Jesus Christ

Although the Bible contains sixty six individual books, together they reveal one magnificent story. From the creation of the world in Genesis to the promise of a new heaven and a new earth in Revelation, Scripture consistently reveals God's love, His faithfulness, and His plan of redemption through Jesus Christ.

Each book contributes something unique. Some record history, others proclaim prophecy, offer wisdom, or encourage believers through letters. Together they reveal God's unchanging character and His desire to draw mankind into a relationship with Himself.

As you study each book, remember that the goal is not simply to finish another lesson. The goal is to know the Author of Scripture more deeply and to allow His Word to transform your life.

For whatsoever things were written aforetime were written for our learning, that we through patience and comfort of the scriptures might have hope.
Romans 15:4

A Great Place to Begin

Start with the Gospel of John

If you are unsure where to begin, the Gospel of John is an excellent place to start. It beautifully introduces the life, ministry, miracles, teachings, death, and resurrection of Jesus Christ while inviting every reader to believe in Him.

After John, continue with the Book of Acts to see how the early church proclaimed the gospel and faithfully followed the Lord.

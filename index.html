About the Book

A Message for Every Heart

Every person has a story.

Some have walked through addiction. Others have experienced adultery, theft, violence, fear, shame, loneliness, or deep rejection. Some have been wounded by the world. Others have been wounded by the church. Some identify as LGBTQIA+. Others have struggled with pride, self righteousness, anger, greed, or hidden sin.

No matter our background, every one of us has something in common.

We all need a Savior.

Who Do You Call Savior? is not about deciding whose sins are worse. It is about the One whose blood is greater than every sin. Jesus Christ did not come to save only those with a respectable past. He came to seek and save the lost, offering forgiveness, redemption, and new life to everyone who comes to Him in faith.

The ground at the foot of the cross is level. No one earns God's grace, and no one is beyond His reach. The invitation of the gospel is extended to every person because salvation is found in Jesus Christ alone.

This book is an invitation to look beyond labels, beyond opinions, beyond condemnation, and beyond your past. It is an invitation to meet the Savior who still transforms lives today.

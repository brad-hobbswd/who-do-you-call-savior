Who Do You Call Savior?

Epilogue

Who Do You Call Savior?

Who Do You Call Savior?

As you come to the final pages of this book, you have reached the place where every journey through the gospel ultimately leads. Throughout these chapters we have explored who Jesus Christ is, why every person needs a Savior, how forgiveness is found through His blood, what it means to be saved, how believers can rest in His promises, and why His invitation still stands today. Every chapter has pointed toward one central truth.

Jesus Christ is the Savior. The question is whether He is your Savior.

The title of this book was never intended to be merely a name on the cover. It was written as a question for every heart. Long after the final page has been read, that question remains.

Who do you call Savior?

This is not a question that can be answered by family tradition, church attendance, religious knowledge, or the opinions of others. It is answered in the quiet place of the heart where every person stands before God. One day every voice will be silent except His. Every earthly accomplishment will fade away. Every excuse will disappear. What will remain is our response to Jesus Christ.

Throughout history, millions have answered that question in different ways. Some have ignored Him. Others have admired Him from a distance. Many have respected Him as a teacher or a prophet. Yet the Scriptures reveal Him as far more than any of these. He is the Lamb of God who takes away the sin of the world. He is the risen Lord who conquered death. He is the only Savior given to humanity.

Throughout the Gospels we repeatedly see Jesus welcoming those whom others had rejected. Society often measured people by their failures, their reputation, or their past. Jesus saw something different. He saw men and women created in the image of God who desperately needed the mercy that only He could provide. Wherever He traveled, His compassion reached those who believed they had been forgotten.

Tax collectors found acceptance in His presence. Fishermen were called to become disciples. Those bound by sickness experienced healing. The blind received sight, the lame walked again, and broken lives were restored through His power. Yet the greatest miracle was not physical healing. It was the forgiveness of sins and the gift of new life that Jesus came to offer.

"For the Son of man is come to seek and to save that which was lost."
Luke 19:10

That mission has never changed. Jesus did not come to condemn those who recognized their need for Him. He came to seek those who were lost and to provide the salvation that only He could accomplish. His love reached beyond social barriers, personal failures, and religious traditions. Every person who came to Him in faith discovered the same truth. The Savior was willing to receive them.

Perhaps you sometimes wonder whether God's invitation truly includes you. You may look back over your life and remember failures you wish you could erase. Regret may whisper that you have wandered too far or sinned too greatly to ever experience God's mercy. Many people carry these burdens in silence, believing they are somehow beyond the reach of His grace.

The Scriptures tell a very different story. Again and again we find Jesus extending mercy to those who believed their lives were beyond hope. The woman who had lived a sinful life found forgiveness instead of condemnation. Zacchaeus discovered that salvation had come to his house. The thief on the cross received the promise of paradise in the final moments of his life. These accounts remind us that the grace of God is greater than the failures of humanity.

"Him that cometh to me I will in no wise cast out."
John 6:37

What an incredible promise. Jesus does not say that He will receive only those with perfect pasts or flawless lives. He promises that the one who comes to Him will never be turned away. His invitation is not limited by age, background, nationality, education, or previous mistakes. The only requirement is that we come to Him in faith.

This truth fills the gospel with hope. No one is beyond the reach of God's grace. No life is too broken for the Savior to restore. No sinner is so far away that Christ cannot forgive, redeem, and make all things new. The same Lord who called people to Himself during His earthly ministry continues to extend that gracious invitation today.

The invitation of Jesus Christ is not merely a message from the past. It is the living call of the gospel that continues to reach hearts today. Every generation has faced the same decision. Every individual must answer the same question. Will I respond to the Savior, or will I turn away from His invitation? God does not force anyone to come to Him, but He lovingly calls every person to receive the salvation He has provided through His Son.

The urgency of this invitation should never be overlooked. Life is uncertain, and tomorrow is never guaranteed. Many people convince themselves that they will seek God someday, after life becomes less busy or after they have accomplished their own plans. Yet the Bible continually reminds us that salvation is an invitation to be received today, not postponed until a more convenient time.

"(For he saith, I have heard thee in a time accepted, and in the day of salvation have I succoured thee: behold, now is the accepted time; behold, now is the day of salvation.)"
2 Corinthians 6:2

God's invitation is both gracious and urgent. His patience is a demonstration of His mercy, giving people opportunity to repent and believe the gospel. Yet His patience should never be mistaken for permission to delay. Every passing day is another reminder of the kindness of God, who continues calling people to Himself while the door of grace remains open.

Responding to Christ is more than acknowledging that He exists or agreeing with the facts of the gospel. It is placing our complete trust in Him as Lord and Savior. It is turning from confidence in ourselves and resting entirely in His finished work on the cross. The gospel calls us into a living relationship with Jesus Christ, not merely into a system of religious beliefs.

This invitation reaches every person without distinction. Whether someone has spent a lifetime searching for truth or has only recently begun asking questions about God, the message remains unchanged. The Savior still calls. His mercy is still available. His grace is still sufficient. His promises are just as certain today as they were when He first spoke them.

"For whosoever shall call upon the name of the Lord shall be saved."
Romans 10:13

The word whosoever is one of the most hope filled words in all of Scripture. It reminds us that the invitation of the gospel extends across every nation, every generation, every culture, and every circumstance. No one is excluded because of a sinful past, and no one is accepted because of personal merit. Every person comes to God the same way, through Jesus Christ.

This has always been the heart of God's plan of redemption. The Savior who welcomed the weary, forgave sinners, healed the broken, and restored the hopeless continues to call people today. His invitation has not changed because His love has not changed. His grace has not diminished. His power to save has not weakened. Jesus Christ is still seeking those who will come to Him in faith.

The invitation of Jesus Christ deserves a response. Throughout the Gospels, every person who encountered Him was faced with a decision. Some believed and followed Him with joyful faith. Others admired His teaching but refused to surrender their lives. Still others rejected Him altogether. Although the responses were different, the invitation remained the same. Jesus called each person to come to Him.

That same invitation continues today. Every reader of this book must eventually answer the same question. What will I do with Jesus Christ? No one else can answer that question for us. Family members cannot believe on our behalf. Friends cannot receive salvation for us. Church attendance, religious traditions, and good intentions cannot take the place of a personal response to the Savior.

"Seek ye the LORD while he may be found, call ye upon him while he is near: Let the wicked forsake his way, and the unrighteous man his thoughts: and let him return unto the LORD, and he will have mercy upon him; and to our God, for he will abundantly pardon."
Isaiah 55:6–7

What a remarkable promise. God does not merely tolerate those who come to Him. He welcomes them with mercy. He does not offer partial forgiveness or reluctant acceptance. He abundantly pardons those who turn to Him in sincere faith. The grace of God is greater than every sin, stronger than every failure, and sufficient for every person who comes to Jesus Christ.

Some people hesitate because they believe they must first change themselves before coming to Christ. The gospel teaches the opposite. We do not come because we have already become worthy. We come because we recognize that we cannot save ourselves. Jesus Christ receives those who place their trust in Him, and through His grace He begins the lifelong work of transforming them into His likeness.

Others hesitate because they fear they have waited too long. They wonder whether years of rebellion or indifference have placed them beyond God's forgiveness. The pages of Scripture continually answer that fear with hope. As long as God's invitation is being extended, His mercy remains available. The Savior still receives those who come to Him with humble, believing hearts.

For those who already belong to Jesus Christ, this invitation should never lose its wonder. The gospel is not only the message that brought us to salvation. It is the message that sustains us every day. We continue coming to Christ for strength, wisdom, peace, grace, and fellowship because He remains everything we need. The Savior who first called us continues to walk faithfully with us throughout every season of life.

"Jesus Christ the same yesterday, and to day, and for ever."
Hebrews 13:8

Because Jesus Christ never changes, His invitation never changes. His love remains steadfast. His mercy remains abundant. His promises remain certain. The same Savior who welcomed weary souls two thousand years ago continues to welcome every person who comes to Him today. His arms remain open, His grace remains sufficient, and His salvation remains available to all who believe.

As we conclude this final chapter, one truth stands above every other. The invitation of Jesus Christ has never changed because the heart of Jesus Christ has never changed. From the beginning of His earthly ministry until this very moment, He continues calling people to Himself with grace, mercy, and truth. The gospel is not merely a message to admire. It is an invitation to receive.

The Savior still calls. The question is whether you will answer.

Throughout this book we have considered who Jesus Christ is, why every person needs a Savior, how forgiveness is found through His blood, what it means to be saved, how believers can rest in His promises, and why our assurance belongs in Him alone. Every chapter has pointed to one unchanging conclusion. Jesus Christ is the only Savior, and His invitation is extended to every person.

Perhaps you have followed Jesus Christ for many years. If so, let this chapter remind you of the grace that first found you. Never lose your wonder at the mercy that rescued your soul. Continue walking faithfully with the Savior who has promised never to leave you nor forsake you. Let your life become a testimony of His goodness and a witness to those who are still searching.

Perhaps you have read these pages with questions still remaining. You may have wondered whether Jesus would truly receive someone like you. You may feel the weight of past sins, broken relationships, unanswered questions, or years spent living apart from God. Hear the invitation of Christ once more. He has not turned you away. His grace is still sufficient. His mercy is still available. His invitation still stands.

"And the Spirit and the bride say, Come. And let him that heareth say, Come. And let him that is athirst come. And whosoever will, let him take the water of life freely."
Revelation 22:17

The Bible closes with an invitation rather than a farewell. God continues calling people to Himself through the gospel of Jesus Christ. His desire is that men and women everywhere would turn to Him in faith and receive the salvation that He alone provides. The same voice that called weary fishermen beside the Sea of Galilee still calls weary hearts today.

One day the opportunity to respond will come to an end, but today His invitation remains. Today His grace is extended. Today His mercy is available. Today salvation is offered through Jesus Christ. There has never been another Savior, and there never will be.

"Neither is there salvation in any other: for there is none other name under heaven given among men, whereby we must be saved."
Acts 4:12

As you turn the page to the Epilogue, do not simply remember the chapters you have read. Remember the Savior they proclaim. Every page has been written with one purpose: to direct your eyes to Jesus Christ. He alone forgives sin. He alone gives eternal life. He alone reconciles us to God. He alone is worthy of our faith, our worship, and our lives.

One Final Invitation

The Journey Continues

The invitation of Jesus Christ has never changed. His grace has not diminished, His mercy has not ended, and His salvation is still offered to every person who comes to Him in faith. As you continue to the Epilogue, return once more to the question that has echoed throughout this book and consider your answer with all your heart. Who do you call Savior?

Who Do You Call Savior?

Epilogue

Who Do You Call Savior?

Who Do You Call Savior?

Every journey has a destination, and every story eventually reaches its final page. As you come to the end of this book, my hope is not simply that you have gained new knowledge, but that you have been drawn closer to Jesus Christ. These pages were never written merely to inform your mind. They were written to point your heart toward the only Savior the world has ever known.

The journey ends where it began. Everything points to Jesus Christ.

From the opening chapter until this final reflection, one truth has remained constant. Humanity's greatest need is not found in wealth, success, education, influence, or religion. Our greatest need is reconciliation with God, and that reconciliation is found only through Jesus Christ. Every chapter has returned to this truth because every page of Scripture returns to Him.

Perhaps some chapters encouraged you. Perhaps others challenged long held assumptions or caused you to think more deeply about your own relationship with God. Whatever your experience has been, I pray this book has accomplished one purpose above all others. I pray it has helped you see Jesus Christ more clearly.

The title of this book has always been more than a title. It has been a question waiting patiently for every reader to answer. That question remains just as important now as it was on the very first page.

Who do you call Savior?

The answer to that question cannot be borrowed from someone else. It cannot be inherited from our parents, secured through church attendance, or established by religious traditions. One day every person will stand before God individually. On that day, our response to Jesus Christ will matter more than every earthly accomplishment, every possession, and every opinion that once seemed so important.

Throughout history people have offered many different answers to the question of who Jesus is. Some have called Him a good teacher. Others have admired Him as a prophet, a moral example, or a remarkable historical figure. Yet the Scriptures reveal something far greater. Jesus Christ is the Son of God, the Lamb who was slain for the sins of the world, the risen Lord who conquered death, and the only Savior through whom we can be reconciled to God.

"Jesus saith unto him, I am the way, the truth, and the life: no man cometh unto the Father, but by me."
John 14:6

Those words leave no room for uncertainty. Jesus did not claim to be one way among many. He declared Himself to be the way. He alone bridges the gap that sin has created between humanity and God. Every promise of forgiveness, every hope of eternal life, and every assurance of salvation rests upon Him alone.

Perhaps you have already placed your faith in Jesus Christ. If so, continue following Him with your whole heart. Remain faithful to His Word. Walk humbly in His grace. Share His gospel with others, and never lose your gratitude for the mercy He has shown you.

Perhaps you are still searching. You may have questions that have not yet been answered or struggles that still weigh heavily upon your heart. Know this with confidence. Jesus Christ has not stopped calling people to Himself. His invitation remains open, His grace remains sufficient, and His mercy is still available to everyone who comes to Him in sincere faith.

No book can save your soul. No author can change your heart. Only Jesus Christ can forgive your sins, make you a new creation, and give you the hope of everlasting life. My prayer is not that you would remember these pages, but that you would remember the Savior they proclaim.

As this journey comes to an end, I want to leave you with one final encouragement. Keep your eyes on Jesus Christ. The world will offer countless voices competing for your attention. Circumstances will change. Trials will come. Questions may arise, and there will be moments when your faith is tested. In every season of life, return to the Savior. He is faithful yesterday, today, and forever.

Do not allow this book to become the destination. If these pages have served any purpose, let them be a signpost directing you to the living Word of God. Continue reading the Scriptures. Continue seeking the Lord in prayer. Continue gathering with believers who love His truth. Continue growing in grace and in the knowledge of Jesus Christ.

"Looking unto Jesus the author and finisher of our faith."
Hebrews 12:2

There will never come a day when you no longer need the Savior. Every step of the Christian life is dependent upon His grace. He strengthens the weary, comforts the brokenhearted, forgives the repentant, and faithfully leads those who trust Him. The One who called you is the One who will continue to guide you until the day you stand in His presence.

If you have not yet placed your faith in Jesus Christ, I urge you not to ignore His invitation. The grace of God is being extended today. The Savior still calls. Do not wait for a more convenient season or imagine there will always be another opportunity. Come to Him while His invitation is before you. Trust Him completely. You will never regret placing your life into the hands of the One who gave His life for you.

If you already know Him, let your life reflect His love. Be quick to forgive. Walk in humility. Serve others with compassion. Speak the truth with gentleness. Share the hope of the gospel with those who have never heard or who have forgotten that Jesus Christ still saves. The world does not simply need better arguments. It needs the Savior.

Long after this book has been set aside, may the question on its cover continue to echo within your heart. Let it shape your decisions, strengthen your faith, and deepen your worship. In every season, through every trial and every joy, may your answer remain the same.

Jesus Christ is my Savior. He is my hope, my peace, and my eternal life.

"He which testifieth these things saith, Surely I come quickly. Amen. Even so, come, Lord Jesus."
Revelation 22:20

Thank you for allowing me to walk with you through these pages. Whether you have read this book in a single sitting or one chapter at a time, I count it a privilege to have shared this journey with you. My prayer has been simple from the very beginning: that every page would point beyond the author and toward the Savior.

If this book has encouraged you, strengthened your faith, answered your questions, or helped you see Jesus Christ more clearly, then all glory belongs to Him. He alone is worthy of our praise. He alone deserves our worship. He alone is the Savior who transforms lives and gives the hope of everlasting life.

When you close this book, do not let your pursuit of Christ end here. Open the Scriptures. Spend time in prayer. Walk daily with the Lord. Worship Him with your whole heart. Share His gospel with those around you, and never lose sight of the grace that first called you to Himself.

The final page of this book is not the end of your journey. If you know Jesus Christ, your greatest days are still ahead. Every step you take in faith brings you closer to the day when you will see your Savior face to face. Until that glorious day, remain faithful, remain hopeful, and remain anchored in His unchanging Word.

One Final Question

Who Do You Call Savior?

The journey ends where it began. Every chapter has pointed to Jesus Christ, and every page has led to this one question. May your answer never be based upon the opinions of others or the traditions of men, but upon the unchanging truth of God's Word. Jesus Christ is the only Savior. May you know Him, follow Him, and faithfully proclaim Him until He comes.

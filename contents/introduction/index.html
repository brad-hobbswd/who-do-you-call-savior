Who Do You Call Savior?

Introduction

Every story is different. Every soul needs the same Savior.

Every Story is Different. Every Soul Needs the Same Savior.

Before you begin reading, I would like to ask you one simple question.

Who do you call Savior?

It is a simple question, yet it is the most important one any person will ever answer. It reaches beyond our backgrounds, our opinions, our successes, our failures, and even our deepest wounds. Before anyone asks where you attend church, what denomination you belong to, or what your past looks like, there is one question that stands above them all.

Who do you call Savior?

Every person has a story. No two stories are exactly alike. Some are filled with joy and faith. Others are marked by pain, broken relationships, addiction, fear, loneliness, regret, disappointment, or shame. Some wounds are visible for everyone to see, while others remain hidden deep within the heart.

Some people have been welcomed by the church with love and compassion. Others have experienced rejection, misunderstanding, or judgment. Some have faithfully followed Christ for many years, while others wonder whether God could ever forgive them or whether there is still hope for their lives.

Although our stories are different, our greatest need is the same.

We all need Jesus Christ.

This book is not about comparing sins or deciding whose life has been more broken. It is not about measuring one person's failures against another's or determining who deserves God's grace. At the foot of the cross, the ground is level. Every one of us stands before God in need of His mercy, His grace, and His salvation.

That is the beauty of the gospel. Jesus Christ did not come only for those who appear to have everything together. He came for people exactly like us.

He came for the weary.

He came for the broken.

He came for those burdened by guilt and shame.

He came for those searching for hope.

He came to seek and to save those who are lost.

His invitation has never changed. He still calls people to Himself with grace, mercy, forgiveness, and the promise of new life.

Throughout these pages, we will explore what it truly means to know Jesus Christ as Savior. Together, we will consider questions that countless people have asked throughout their lives, questions that shape not only our understanding of God, but also the direction of our lives and our eternal hope.

Can I really be forgiven?

Can God redeem someone like me?

How can I know that I am saved?

What does it truly mean to follow Jesus Christ?

These are not merely theological questions to be debated. They are deeply personal questions that every heart must eventually answer. The answers we find in God's Word shape not only how we live today, but where we will spend eternity.

As you read this book, my prayer is that you will do more than simply turn its pages. I pray you will open your Bible, search the Scriptures for yourself, and allow the Word of God to speak to your heart. Do not accept my words simply because they are printed here. Measure everything against the truth of Scripture, and let Jesus Christ be your Teacher.

This book does not claim to answer every question or resolve every struggle you may face. Instead, it is an invitation to look beyond human opinions and place your confidence in the One who never changes.

If, after reading these pages, you remember my name but fail to remember Jesus Christ, then I have failed in my purpose.

But if this book helps you know Him more deeply, trust Him more completely, love Him more faithfully, and follow Him more wholeheartedly, then every page will have been written for a worthwhile purpose.

Thank you for allowing me to walk beside you for a little while. It is my sincere prayer that this journey strengthens your faith, encourages your heart, and reminds you that the invitation of Jesus Christ is still extended to every person today.

Now, let us begin the journey together.

Neither is there salvation in any other: for there is none other name under heaven given among men, whereby we must be saved.
Acts 4:12

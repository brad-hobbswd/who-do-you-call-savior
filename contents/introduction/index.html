Who Do You Call Savior?

Introduction

Every Story is Different. Every Soul Needs the Same Savior.

Every Story is Different. Every Soul Needs the Same Savior.

Before you begin reading, I would like to ask you a question.

Who do you call Savior?

It is a simple question, yet it is the most important one any person will ever answer. It reaches beyond our backgrounds, opinions, successes, failures, and even our deepest wounds. Before anyone asks where you attend church, what denomination you belong to, or what your past looks like, there is one question that stands above them all.

Who do you call Savior?

Every person has a story.

Some stories are filled with joy and faith. Others are marked by pain, addiction, broken relationships, fear, loneliness, regret, or disappointment. Some people carry scars that everyone can see. Others carry wounds that remain hidden beneath the surface. Some have been embraced by the church, while others have experienced judgment, rejection, or have been convinced they could never belong.

No matter how different our stories may be, every one of us shares the same need.

We need Jesus Christ.

This book is not about comparing sins or deciding whose life has been more broken. It is not about placing one person's failures above another's or measuring ourselves against those around us. The ground at the foot of the cross is level. We all stand before God in need of His grace, His mercy, and His salvation.

The good news of the gospel is that Jesus Christ came for people exactly like us.

He came for the weary.

He came for the broken.

He came for those burdened by guilt and shame.

He came for those searching for hope.

He came to seek and to save those who are lost.

Throughout these pages, we will explore what it truly means to know Jesus Christ as Savior. Together, we will consider questions that many people quietly carry in their hearts.

Can I really be forgiven?

Can God redeem someone like me?

How can I know I am saved?

What does it truly mean to follow Jesus Christ?

These are not merely theological questions. They are deeply personal because the answers shape both our present lives and our eternal destiny.

My prayer is that this book will encourage you to open your Bible, search the Scriptures, and seek Jesus Christ with sincerity. Do not accept my words simply because they are written on these pages. Let the Word of God be your guide, and allow His truth to speak to your heart.

If, after reading this book, you remember my name but forget Jesus Christ, then I have failed.

But if these pages help you know Him more deeply, trust Him more completely, and love Him more faithfully, then this book has fulfilled its purpose.

Thank you for opening these pages and allowing me to walk beside you for a little while.

Now, let us begin together.

Neither is there salvation in any other: for there is none other name under heaven given among men, whereby we must be saved.
Acts 4:12

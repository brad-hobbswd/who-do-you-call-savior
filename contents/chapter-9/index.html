Chapter Nine

When Others Question Your Salvation

When Others Question Your Salvation

Sooner or later, many believers experience one of the most painful challenges in their walk with Jesus Christ. Someone questions the sincerity of their faith. Sometimes the criticism comes from strangers. At other times it comes from friends, family members, or even fellow Christians. Words spoken carelessly or harshly can leave lasting wounds, causing sincere believers to wonder whether their relationship with God is genuine.

People may judge, assume, and condemn. Jesus Christ alone is the Savior.

The opinions of people have always been divided. During His earthly ministry, Jesus Himself was misunderstood, criticized, rejected, and falsely accused. Some believed He was the promised Messiah, while others called Him a deceiver. The same crowds that praised Him one day demanded His crucifixion only a few days later. Human opinion has never been a reliable measure of divine truth.

Because followers of Jesus belong to Him, they should not be surprised when they experience misunderstanding as well. Faithfulness to Christ will not always be applauded by the world, and sincere believers may even find themselves questioned by those within religious circles. While loving correction rooted in Scripture should always be received with humility, accusations built upon assumptions, traditions, or personal opinions should never replace the authority of God's Word.

"Judge not according to the appearance, but judge righteous judgment."
John 7:24

Jesus taught that righteous judgment must be guided by truth rather than outward appearances. Human beings can observe actions and hear words, but only God fully knows the heart. We may misunderstand another person's motives, overlook God's work in someone's life, or reach conclusions based upon incomplete information. The Lord alone sees perfectly, and His judgment is always just.

For that reason, believers must be careful not to allow the opinions of others to become the foundation of their assurance. Our confidence cannot rest upon whether people approve of us or question us. It must rest upon Jesus Christ and the unchanging promises of His Word.

When our salvation is questioned, our first response should not be fear or defensiveness. Instead, we should return to the Word of God. The Scriptures are the final authority in matters of faith and salvation. Human opinions change, but God's truth remains constant. If our confidence is built upon the promises of Christ, it will remain steady even when others misunderstand or criticize us.

This does not mean that believers should ignore every concern raised by others. The Bible encourages honest self examination and humble hearts that are willing to receive correction when it is grounded in Scripture. God often uses faithful pastors, mature believers, and loving brothers and sisters in Christ to help us grow. Wise correction is a gift from God, not a threat to our faith.

The danger comes when we allow personal opinions, traditions, or assumptions to become greater authorities than God's Word. A person may sincerely believe that someone is not saved because they worship differently, come from another background, or have not followed the same spiritual journey. While these opinions may be strongly held, they cannot determine a person's standing before God. Only the Lord knows every heart.

"But the LORD said unto Samuel, Look not on his countenance, or on the height of his stature; because I have refused him: for the LORD seeth not as man seeth; for man looketh on the outward appearance, but the LORD looketh on the heart."
1 Samuel 16:7

This truth should produce both humility and confidence. It reminds us not to make careless judgments about others while also reminding us that our standing before God is not determined by human observation. People see only a small part of our lives. God sees every thought, every motive, every struggle, every victory, and every sincere act of faith. His knowledge is perfect, while ours is limited.

The Apostle Paul understood what it meant to be misunderstood. Throughout his ministry he faced criticism from religious leaders, false teachers, and even fellow believers who questioned his motives and authority. Yet Paul refused to build his confidence upon the approval of people. His desire was to remain faithful to Jesus Christ, knowing that the Lord alone would judge his life with perfect righteousness.

"For do I now persuade men, or God? or do I seek to please men? for if I yet pleased men, I should not be the servant of Christ."
Galatians 1:10

Paul's words remind every believer of an important truth. If our goal is to satisfy every person around us, we will never experience lasting peace. Someone will always disagree with us, misunderstand us, or question our faith. Our calling is not to win the approval of everyone we meet. Our calling is to faithfully follow Jesus Christ according to His Word.

When our assurance rests in Christ rather than in public opinion, we are free to walk with humility and confidence at the same time. We remain teachable because we know we have not arrived at perfection. At the same time, we refuse to allow unfounded accusations or unfair judgments to steal the peace that God has given through His promises.

There is another danger believers should avoid when others question their salvation. In an effort to defend themselves, some become discouraged and begin measuring their relationship with God by the opinions of people instead of the testimony of Scripture. Others become angry or defensive, refusing to examine themselves honestly. Neither response reflects the wisdom found in God's Word. The Lord calls us to humility without surrendering the assurance that He provides.

The Apostle Paul encouraged believers to examine themselves, not to create fear, but to ensure that their faith rested upon Jesus Christ. Biblical self examination is healthy because it drives us back to the Savior. Rather than asking whether we meet someone else's expectations, we ask whether our lives are surrendered to Christ and whether our confidence rests in Him alone.

"Examine yourselves, whether ye be in the faith; prove your own selves."
2 Corinthians 13:5

This examination should always be guided by Scripture. We are not called to compare ourselves with other believers or to measure our faith by another person's spiritual journey. God works uniquely in every life. While the evidence of salvation is consistent with His Word, each believer grows at a different pace under the faithful care of the Great Shepherd.

When criticism comes, it is wise to ask whether there is any biblical truth that should be received. If someone lovingly points out an area where we need to grow according to the Scriptures, we should thank God for that correction and respond with humility. Such correction is an act of love that helps believers mature in their walk with Christ.

If, however, the criticism is rooted in misunderstanding, personal preference, tradition, or assumptions rather than the Word of God, we should not allow it to rob us of the peace Christ has given. The opinions of people cannot overturn the promises of God. What He has declared in His Word remains true regardless of what others may say.

"Who art thou that judgest another man's servant? to his own master he standeth or falleth. Yea, he shall be holden up: for God is able to make him stand."
Romans 14:4

Paul reminds us that every believer ultimately belongs to the Lord. Jesus Christ is our Master, our Shepherd, and our Judge. While we are accountable to encourage one another in truth, no person has the authority to pronounce eternal judgment upon another believer's heart. God alone possesses that authority because He alone knows perfectly those who belong to Him.

This truth should produce both peace and humility. Peace comes from knowing that our standing before God is secured through Jesus Christ rather than through human approval. Humility reminds us that we must also avoid making careless judgments about the salvation of others. Just as we desire grace when others misunderstand us, we should extend that same grace while remaining faithful to the truth of God's Word.

The Christian life was never meant to be lived under the constant weight of human opinion. It was meant to be lived under the lordship of Jesus Christ. As we continue trusting Him, obeying His Word, and growing in His grace, our confidence remains anchored in the One who knows us completely and loves us perfectly.

Perhaps the greatest comfort for every believer is knowing that Jesus Christ is both our Savior and our Advocate. The One who died for our sins is also the One who intercedes for His people. When accusations arise, whether from others or from our own troubled hearts, we are not left to defend ourselves alone. We have a Savior who has already secured our redemption through His perfect sacrifice.

"Who shall lay any thing to the charge of God's elect? It is God that justifieth. Who is he that condemneth? It is Christ that died, yea rather, that is risen again, who is even at the right hand of God, who also maketh intercession for us."
Romans 8:33–34

These words offer tremendous assurance. If God has declared a sinner righteous through faith in Jesus Christ, no human accusation can overturn His verdict. People may misunderstand us. They may question our motives or doubt the sincerity of our faith. Yet the final judgment belongs to God alone, and He has provided complete justification through His Son.

This does not mean believers should become prideful or unwilling to receive biblical correction. Humility remains one of the marks of genuine faith. Those who truly love Christ desire to grow, to learn, and to become more like Him. They welcome correction that is rooted in Scripture because they recognize that God uses His Word to shape their lives.

At the same time, believers must refuse to live under the constant fear of human condemnation. Some people will never be satisfied regardless of how faithfully we seek to follow Christ. Others may judge according to traditions, preferences, or outward appearances rather than according to the truth of God's Word. If our peace depends upon pleasing every person, we will never enjoy the freedom Christ has purchased for us.

Jesus Himself warned His followers that they would face misunderstanding because they belong to Him. The world rejected its Savior before it rejected His disciples. Faithfulness has never been measured by popularity or acceptance. It has always been measured by obedience to God.

"If the world hate you, ye know that it hated me before it hated you."
John 15:18

Knowing this allows believers to respond differently when questioned or criticized. Instead of reacting with bitterness, we can answer with gentleness. Instead of seeking revenge, we can pray for those who misunderstand us. Instead of becoming discouraged, we can continue following Christ with confidence, knowing that our identity is found in Him rather than in the opinions of others.

Our greatest desire should never be to prove ourselves right in the eyes of people. Our greatest desire should be to faithfully honor Jesus Christ. When He is our focus, criticism loses its power to control our hearts. We become free to serve Him with humility, courage, and peace because our confidence rests in His approval above every other voice.

The believer who understands this truth discovers a wonderful freedom. Human opinions may come and go, but the love of Christ remains constant. The judgments of people may change from one day to the next, but the promises of God never fail. Because Jesus Christ is faithful, His people can walk forward with confidence, trusting Him in every circumstance.

As we bring this chapter to a close, remember that your assurance was never intended to rest upon the opinions of people. It rests upon the person and work of Jesus Christ. The voices around you may change. Some will encourage you, others may discourage you, and still others may question your relationship with God. Yet none of those voices has the authority to determine your standing before Him. That authority belongs to God alone.

The loudest voice is not always the truest voice. The voice that matters most is the voice of God revealed through His Word.

When others question your salvation, return to the Scriptures. Let God's promises speak louder than human opinions. Remember the faithfulness of Christ, the witness of the Holy Spirit, and the transforming work God has begun within your life. These are the foundations of biblical assurance. They point you back to the Savior rather than to yourself or to the judgments of others.

There may be times when criticism reveals an area where growth is needed. Receive that correction with humility and gratitude if it is rooted in God's Word. The Lord lovingly disciplines His children because He desires their spiritual maturity. Correction that comes from Scripture is one of the ways God shapes believers into the likeness of His Son.

At other times, criticism will be unfair, inaccurate, or based upon assumptions. In those moments, resist the temptation to become bitter or defensive. Continue walking faithfully with Jesus Christ. Your responsibility is not to satisfy every opinion but to remain faithful to the One who called you by His grace.

"And let us not be weary in well doing: for in due season we shall reap, if we faint not."
Galatians 6:9

Faithfulness often requires perseverance. There will always be voices competing for your attention, but the believer who remains anchored in God's Word will not be shaken by every accusation or criticism. Christ is the Good Shepherd, and His sheep continue listening to His voice above every other voice.

One day every opinion, every misunderstanding, and every false accusation will pass away. On that day, only one judgment will matter. The believer who has placed his or her faith in Jesus Christ will stand before the Savior who knows every heart perfectly. He sees every struggle, every prayer, every act of obedience, and every step of faith that others may never understand.

Until that day, continue trusting Him. Continue following Him. Continue resting in His promises. The Savior who called you is faithful, and His Word remains true forever.

"Being confident of this very thing, that he which hath begun a good work in you will perform it until the day of Jesus Christ."
Philippians 1:6

Stand Firm in Christ

His Verdict Is the One That Matters

People may question, misunderstand, or even condemn, but Jesus Christ alone is the righteous Judge and the only Savior. As you continue your journey, let your confidence rest not in the approval of people but in the unchanging promises of the One who knows you completely and loves you perfectly.

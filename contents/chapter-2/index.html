Who Do You Call Savior?

Chapter Two

The Ground Is Level at the Cross

The Ground Is Level at the Cross

In the previous chapter, we discovered who Jesus Christ is. Once we know who the Savior is, another question naturally follows.

Why Do I Need a Savior?

Many people believe they are good enough to stand before God. They compare their lives with others and assume that if they have done more good than bad, they have little to fear. Yet the Bible never measures us against one another. It measures every one of us against the holiness of God.

The cross reminds us that no one stands above another. Wealth cannot save us. Success cannot save us. Religion cannot save us. Good works cannot remove our sins. Every person stands before God with the same need.

The ground is level at the cross.

This is one of the most beautiful truths in the gospel. Jesus did not come only for the respected or the religious. He came for every person who would recognize their need for Him. Throughout His ministry, He welcomed the broken, the weary, the forgotten, and the repentant, offering forgiveness and new life through faith in Him.

That does not mean Jesus ignored sin or lowered God's standard of holiness. Instead, He called people to repentance while extending grace to those who came to Him with humble hearts. His mercy never excused sin, but it always offered hope to the sinner.

The Apostle Paul summarized humanity's condition with one simple statement that leaves no room for pride or comparison.

"For all have sinned, and come short of the glory of God."
Romans 3:23

The word all includes every one of us. No one is excluded. Whether rich or poor, young or old, religious or irreligious, every person has sinned and stands in need of the same Savior.

Once we understand that truth, the cross begins to look different. We stop comparing ourselves with others because we realize that every one of us has fallen short of God's perfect standard. Pride gives way to humility, and judgment gives way to compassion.

If salvation depended upon our own goodness, none of us could stand before God with confidence. We would always wonder whether we had done enough. The good news of the gospel is that salvation has never depended upon human effort. It has always depended upon the grace of God.

Grace is God's unearned favor toward those who could never earn it. He offers forgiveness where judgment is deserved, hope where there was despair, and life where sin had brought death. Grace does not minimize sin. It magnifies the love of God by revealing His desire to save sinners through Jesus Christ.

"For by grace are ye saved through faith; and that not of yourselves: it is the gift of God: Not of works, lest any man should boast."
Ephesians 2:8 and 9

These verses remind us that salvation is God's gift. No one earns it, deserves it, or purchases it. Every believer comes to the cross in exactly the same way, trusting completely in Jesus Christ.

"For whosoever shall call upon the name of the Lord shall be saved."
Romans 10:13

The word whosoever is one of the most encouraging words in all of Scripture. It reminds us that God's invitation is open to every person who comes to Him in faith. No matter your past, your failures, or your story, His grace is sufficient. At the foot of the cross, every person stands on equal ground, every sinner needs the same Savior, and every heart is invited to receive the same gift of salvation.

Looking Ahead

Understanding Our Greatest Need

If every person stands equally in need of Jesus Christ, the next question naturally follows. Why do we need a Savior in the first place? To answer that question, we must understand the reality and consequences of sin as revealed throughout God's Word.

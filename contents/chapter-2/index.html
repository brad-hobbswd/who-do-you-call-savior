Who Do You Call Savior?

Chapter Two

The Ground Is Level at the Cross

The Ground Is Level at the Cross

One of the greatest misconceptions about salvation is the belief that some people need God's grace more than others. Human nature has a tendency to compare lives, measure failures, and rank sins according to our own understanding. We often assume that those whose lives appear respectable stand closer to God than those whose sins are visible to the world. Yet the Scriptures paint an entirely different picture. When we stand before God, every comparison disappears.

The ground is level at the cross.

The cross of Jesus Christ is the great equalizer of humanity. It reminds us that every person, regardless of age, background, nationality, education, wealth, or social standing, shares the same condition before a holy God. We have all been created in His image, yet we have all been affected by the reality of sin. No one stands above another, and no one is beyond the reach of God's mercy.

Throughout history, societies have divided people into countless categories. Some are honored because of their achievements, while others are overlooked because of their failures. Some enjoy positions of influence and authority, while others struggle simply to be noticed. The world constantly measures people by appearance, accomplishments, possessions, and reputation. God's standard, however, is altogether different. He does not evaluate humanity according to outward success but according to the condition of the heart.

This truth is woven throughout the Scriptures. Kings and shepherds, scholars and fishermen, rulers and servants all appear within the pages of the Bible. Their circumstances were vastly different, yet they shared one common need. Every one of them depended upon the grace of God. Their position in society could not earn His favor, nor could their accomplishments remove the stain of sin. Whether rich or poor, respected or rejected, every person stood in need of the same Savior.

This is one of the most beautiful truths found in the gospel. Jesus Christ did not come for one class of people while excluding another. He did not reserve His invitation for those who possessed wealth, influence, or religious prestige. His ministry continually demonstrated that the mercy of God extends to every person who comes to Him in faith. The invitation of salvation is not based upon human worthiness but upon God's grace.

The ministry of Jesus provides countless examples of this truth. He spoke with religious leaders who were respected by their communities, yet He also welcomed tax collectors who had become outcasts because of their dishonesty. He healed those whom society had forgotten, showed compassion to those who were considered unclean, and extended mercy to people whose pasts were marked by failure and shame. Again and again, Jesus demonstrated that God's invitation was never limited to a select few. His compassion reached every person who recognized their need for Him.

This does not mean that Jesus ignored sin or lowered God's standard of holiness. On the contrary, He consistently called people to repentance and a transformed life. His mercy never excused sin, but it always offered hope to the sinner. Wherever Jesus encountered brokenness, He extended an invitation to experience forgiveness, restoration, and new life through faith in Him.

Perhaps no passage expresses this truth more clearly than the words of the Apostle Paul in his letter to the Romans. After carefully explaining the condition of humanity, Paul reached a conclusion that leaves no room for comparison or personal pride.

"For all have sinned, and come short of the glory of God."
Romans 3:23

The word all includes every one of us. It does not distinguish between the religious and the irreligious, the moral and the immoral, or the wealthy and the poor. Every human being has fallen short of God's perfect standard. Sin is not merely the actions we commit but the condition that separates humanity from its Creator. Because of that reality, every person stands in equal need of the Savior.

Recognizing this truth changes the way we see ourselves and the people around us. Pride gives way to humility because no one can claim to have earned God's favor through personal goodness. Judgment gives way to compassion because we remember that we, too, have been recipients of His mercy. Gratitude replaces self righteousness because we understand that our salvation rests entirely upon God's grace rather than our own accomplishments.

The cross reminds us that no one arrives in the presence of God by climbing higher than someone else. We all come by the same path. We come as sinners who have nothing to offer except a heart willing to trust in Jesus Christ. At the foot of the cross, titles lose their importance, achievements fade into the background, and every human distinction gives way to one unchanging truth. We all stand in need of the same Savior.

If salvation depended upon human goodness, no one could stand before God with confidence. If forgiveness were earned through good works, every person would spend a lifetime wondering whether they had done enough. The good news of the gospel is that God's plan of salvation has never been based upon human merit. It has always been rooted in His love, His mercy, and His grace.

Grace is one of the most beautiful words found in Scripture. Simply stated, grace is God's unmerited favor toward those who could never earn it. It is His willingness to extend forgiveness where judgment is deserved, to offer hope where there was once despair, and to provide eternal life where death was the rightful consequence of sin. Grace does not ignore the seriousness of sin, but it reveals the greatness of God's love in providing a way for sinners to be reconciled to Him.

The Apostle Paul reminded believers that salvation is entirely the work of God. It is not a reward for human effort, nor is it something that can be purchased through religious devotion or personal achievement. From beginning to end, salvation is a gift that God freely offers through Jesus Christ.

"For by grace are ye saved through faith; and that not of yourselves: it is the gift of God: Not of works, lest any man should boast."
Ephesians 2:8 and 9

These verses leave no room for pride or self confidence. No one can stand before God and claim to have earned salvation through personal righteousness. Every believer comes to the cross in exactly the same way, relying completely upon the mercy of God revealed through Jesus Christ. The strongest believer and the newest believer stand upon the same foundation. Both have been saved by grace.

This truth should transform the way Christians view one another. The church is not a gathering of people who have achieved spiritual perfection. It is a fellowship of redeemed men and women who have experienced the same mercy and continue to depend upon the same Savior each day. The ground remains level, not only at the moment of salvation, but throughout the entire Christian life.

Understanding grace also changes the way we see those who have not yet come to Christ. Rather than looking upon others with pride or condemnation, we remember that we once stood in the very same place. The difference between the believer and the unbeliever is not that one deserved God's favor while the other did not. The difference is that one has received the grace that God freely offers through Jesus Christ.

The invitation of the gospel has always been extended to every person. Throughout His earthly ministry, Jesus welcomed those who recognized their need for Him. He did not turn away the broken, the weary, or the repentant. Instead, He continually demonstrated that God's mercy is available to everyone who comes to Him with a sincere heart. His invitation crossed every social boundary and reached every kind of person because His mission was to seek and to save those who were lost.

This truth should give hope to every reader. Perhaps you have wondered whether your past has placed you beyond the reach of God's grace. Perhaps you carry regrets that seem impossible to overcome or failures that continue to weigh heavily upon your heart. The message of the gospel reminds us that no one is beyond the reach of Jesus Christ. His grace is greater than our greatest failure, His mercy is deeper than our deepest regret, and His love continues to call people to Himself today.

At the same time, the gospel reminds us that no one should become confident in personal righteousness. Good deeds, religious traditions, family heritage, or years of church attendance cannot remove the guilt of sin. Every person who receives salvation does so in exactly the same way, by placing faith in Jesus Christ and trusting completely in what He has accomplished. There is no higher ground reserved for the respectable and no lower ground assigned to the broken. At the foot of the cross, every person stands on equal ground before God.

When we truly understand this truth, humility becomes the natural response. We stop comparing ourselves with others because we recognize that every believer has been saved by the same grace. We become quicker to forgive because we remember how much we have been forgiven. We extend compassion because we understand that the mercy we have received is the very mercy God desires to extend to others. The gospel not only changes our relationship with God, but it also changes the way we see every person around us.

"For whosoever shall call upon the name of the Lord shall be saved."
Romans 10:13

The word whosoever is one of the most encouraging words found in Scripture. It reminds us that God's invitation is not reserved for a select group of people. It reaches every nation, every generation, every background, and every life. The ground is level at the cross because every person comes to the same Savior, receives the same grace, and is offered the same hope through Jesus Christ.

Looking Ahead

Understanding Our Greatest Need

If every person stands equally in need of Jesus Christ, the next question naturally follows. Why do we need a Savior in the first place? To answer that question, we must understand the reality and consequences of sin as revealed throughout God's Word.

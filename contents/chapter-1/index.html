Who Do You Call Savior?

Chapter One

Who Is Your Savior?

Who Is Your Savior?

Every person eventually reaches a moment when life's biggest questions can no longer be ignored. We wonder why we are here, whether our sins can be forgiven, what happens after death, and whether there is hope beyond this life. Each of these questions leads to one that stands above them all.

Who Is Your Savior?

It may sound like a simple question, but it is the most important question you will ever answer. Before we can understand salvation, forgiveness, or eternal life, we must first understand the One who offers them. Everything the gospel teaches begins with Jesus Christ.

Many people know the name of Jesus, yet not everyone knows who He truly is. Some believe He was a great teacher. Others admire Him as a prophet, a miracle worker, or an influential man in history. While each of those descriptions recognizes something remarkable about Him, the Scriptures reveal something far greater. Jesus Christ is the promised Savior, the Messiah, and the only hope for a world lost in sin.

The identity of Jesus is not a minor doctrine. It is the foundation of the Christian faith. If we misunderstand who He is, we will misunderstand everything He came to accomplish. His teachings, His miracles, His death on the cross, and His resurrection all point to one unchanging truth. Jesus Christ came to seek and to save those who were lost.

Throughout His earthly ministry, people responded to Jesus in different ways. Some believed Him and followed Him with sincere faith. Others questioned Him, rejected Him, or refused to believe despite the miracles they witnessed. Although their opinions differed, every person was confronted with the same question.

Who is Jesus?

More than two thousand years later, that question has not changed. People continue to debate His identity, write books about Him, and form opinions based upon tradition, culture, or personal experience. Yet opinions do not determine truth. Only the Word of God reveals who Jesus truly is.

One of the clearest moments in the Gospels is found in Matthew 16. Jesus had spent many months teaching, healing the sick, performing miracles, and revealing the kingdom of God. As He traveled with His disciples into the region of Caesarea Philippi, He asked them a question that still speaks to every generation.

"When Jesus came into the coasts of Caesarea Philippi, he asked his disciples, saying, Whom do men say that I the Son of man am?"
Matthew 16:13

The disciples answered with what they had been hearing from the crowds. Some believed Jesus was John the Baptist. Others thought He was Elijah or Jeremiah, while still others believed He was one of the prophets. The people recognized that Jesus was extraordinary, but they had not yet understood who He truly was.

The opinions of the crowd were interesting, but they were not the reason Jesus asked the question. He was leading His disciples to something far more personal. After hearing what others believed, He turned to the men who had walked beside Him, listened to His teaching, and witnessed His miracles.

"He saith unto them, But whom say ye that I am?"
Matthew 16:15

Everything changed with that question. Jesus was no longer asking about public opinion. He was asking for a personal confession of faith. His disciples could no longer answer with someone else's beliefs. They had to answer for themselves.

That same question still stands before every person today.

"And Simon Peter answered and said, Thou art the Christ, the Son of the living God."
Matthew 16:16

Peter did not answer with uncertainty or repeat what others believed. He spoke from personal conviction. After walking with Jesus, hearing His teaching, witnessing His miracles, and experiencing His compassion, Peter knew who stood before him.

"Thou art the Christ."

Peter confessed that Jesus was the promised Messiah, the One God had promised from the beginning. Throughout the Old Testament, God revealed that a Redeemer would come to save His people. Every promise, every prophecy, and every sacrifice pointed forward to Jesus Christ.

The identity of Jesus changes everything. Christianity is not built upon traditions, philosophies, or human ideas. It is built upon the person of Jesus Christ. Because of who He is, His words carry authority. Because of who He is, His sacrifice on the cross has the power to forgive sin. Because of who He is, the empty tomb gives every believer the hope of eternal life.

Before we can understand salvation, we must understand the Savior. We cannot separate what Jesus accomplished from who He is. The gospel begins and ends with Him.

Many people want God's blessings without knowing the One who gives them. They seek peace without surrender, forgiveness without repentance, or eternal life without following Christ. The Scriptures never present salvation that way. Salvation begins with knowing Jesus, trusting Him, and believing what God has revealed about Him through His Word.

That is why Jesus asked His disciples this question. He was not searching for information. He already knew what people believed. Instead, He wanted His followers to examine their own hearts. Faith has never been built upon popular opinion. It has always rested upon God's revelation.

Who do you believe Jesus is?

That question cannot be answered by your family, your church, your pastor, or your friends. It cannot be answered by tradition or religious heritage. Every person must answer it personally, because every person will one day stand before God.

The Bible leaves no uncertainty about the answer. Jesus Christ is the promised Messiah, the Lamb of God who takes away the sin of the world, the Good Shepherd who laid down His life for His sheep, and the risen Lord who conquered death through His resurrection. He alone has the authority to forgive sins, reconcile us to God, and give eternal life.

As we continue through this book, we will discover why every person needs a Savior, what sin has done to humanity, and how God's plan of redemption is fulfilled through Jesus Christ. Every chapter builds upon this truth because everything begins with Him.

"Neither is there salvation in any other: for there is none other name under heaven given among men, whereby we must be saved."
Acts 4:12

The message of the Bible is clear. Salvation is not found in a church, a tradition, a religious leader, or our own good works. Salvation is found in Jesus Christ alone. He is the Savior God promised, the sacrifice for our sins, and the only way to the Father.

That truth has never changed. Every generation must answer the same question Jesus asked His disciples. It is not enough to know what others believe. Each of us must decide who Jesus is according to the Word of God.

Who is your Savior?

If your answer is Jesus Christ, then your journey has only begun. Following Him is more than agreeing with a statement of faith. It is a life of repentance, obedience, trust, and continual growth in His Word. The gospel does not simply change what we believe. It changes who we become.

If you are still searching, I encourage you to keep reading with an open Bible and an open heart. Ask God to reveal Himself through His Word. He is faithful to those who sincerely seek Him.

Every chapter that follows will build upon the truth we have established here. Before we can understand sin, repentance, baptism, grace, or eternal life, we must first know the One who makes each of those truths possible.

The Foundation Has Been Laid

The Journey Continues

Now that we have answered the question of who the Savior is, we are ready to discover why every person needs Him. The next chapter begins where every salvation story begins, with the reality of our need for a Savior.

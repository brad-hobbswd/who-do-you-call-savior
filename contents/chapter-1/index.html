Who Do You Call Savior?

Chapter One

Who Is Your Savior?

Who Is Your Savior?

Every person eventually reaches a moment when the most important questions in life can no longer be ignored. Questions about purpose, forgiveness, hope, and eternity have occupied the minds of people throughout every generation. While each of these questions deserves careful consideration, they all lead to one greater question that stands at the very heart of the Christian faith.

Who is your Savior?

At first, the answer may seem obvious. Many people would immediately respond by saying, "Jesus Christ." Yet saying His name and understanding who He truly is are not always the same thing. Throughout history, countless people have spoken the name of Jesus while holding very different beliefs about His identity. Some have admired Him as a wise teacher whose words continue to influence the world. Others have respected Him as a prophet, a miracle worker, or an extraordinary man of compassion. While each of these descriptions recognizes something remarkable about Jesus, none of them fully explains who He is according to the Scriptures.

The Bible introduces Jesus Christ in a way that is unlike any other person in history. He is not presented as merely another religious leader or philosopher whose ideas deserve consideration. From the opening pages of the New Testament, His life fulfills promises that God had spoken centuries before through the prophets. Every event surrounding His birth, ministry, death, burial, and resurrection reveals that His coming was neither accidental nor unexpected. It was the fulfillment of God's eternal plan to redeem humanity.

Understanding the identity of Jesus Christ is far more than an intellectual exercise. It is the foundation upon which every other truth in the Christian faith is built. Before we can understand the message of salvation, the meaning of repentance, the purpose of the cross, or the hope of eternal life, we must first understand the One who accomplished them. If our understanding of Jesus is incomplete, our understanding of the gospel will also be incomplete.

This is why the New Testament repeatedly directs our attention to the person of Jesus Christ before explaining the work He came to accomplish. His miracles demonstrated His authority. His teachings revealed the heart of God. His compassion displayed God's love for humanity. His death satisfied the penalty for sin, and His resurrection declared His victory over death itself. Every part of His earthly ministry points to the same conclusion: Jesus Christ is the Savior whom God promised from the very beginning.

During His ministry, people responded to Jesus in many different ways. Some followed Him with sincere faith, believing that He was the promised Messiah. Others were drawn by the miracles they witnessed and the hope of receiving physical healing. Religious leaders often questioned His authority and sought opportunities to challenge His teaching, while many others remained undecided, uncertain of what they believed about Him. Although their responses varied, they all had one thing in common. Every person was forced to consider who Jesus truly was.

That reality has not changed. More than two thousand years later, the world continues to discuss Jesus Christ. Books have been written about Him, documentaries have examined His life, scholars have debated His claims, and countless opinions have been offered concerning His identity. Yet despite the many voices that continue to speak about Him, there remains only one source that reveals Him with complete truth.

That source is the Word of God.

One of the clearest examples of this is found in Matthew 16. After spending considerable time with His disciples, Jesus led them into the region of Caesarea Philippi. Surrounded by cities known for pagan worship and competing religious beliefs, He asked a question that seemed simple on the surface but carried eternal significance. Rather than beginning with what His disciples believed, Jesus first asked what they had been hearing from the people around them.

"When Jesus came into the coasts of Caesarea Philippi, he asked his disciples, saying, Whom do men say that I the Son of man am?"
Matthew 16:13

The disciples answered by repeating the opinions they had heard from the crowds. Some believed Jesus was John the Baptist. Others thought He was Elijah returned from heaven. Still others suggested that He was Jeremiah or one of the prophets. These responses revealed something important about the people who had watched Jesus teach, heal, and perform miracles. They recognized that He was extraordinary, yet they had failed to understand who He truly was.

Human opinion has always been divided when it comes to Jesus Christ. During His earthly ministry, people observed the same miracles and listened to the same teaching, yet they reached very different conclusions. Some believed, while others doubted. Some worshiped Him, while others rejected Him. The difference was never found in the evidence that Jesus provided but in the willingness of each person to believe what God had revealed.

Although public opinion was interesting, it was not the reason Jesus asked the question. He was preparing His disciples for something much more personal. After allowing them to share what others believed, He turned the conversation toward those who knew Him best. The question was no longer about the crowds. It was about the hearts of the men who had walked beside Him every day.

"He saith unto them, But whom say ye that I am?"
Matthew 16:15

With those few words, Jesus transformed the conversation from one of public opinion to one of personal conviction. The disciples could no longer answer by repeating what they had heard from others. They were now being asked to declare what they themselves believed after witnessing His life and ministry. It was a question that demanded an honest answer, and it is a question that continues to confront every person today.

The world will always have opinions about Jesus Christ. Cultures change, philosophies evolve, and ideas come and go, but the question remains unchanged. Every generation must answer it for themselves. Every individual must decide whether Jesus is simply another figure in history or whether He is truly the Savior revealed in the Scriptures.

The question Jesus asked His disciples has never become outdated. It is just as relevant in the twenty first century as it was when it was first spoken in Caesarea Philippi. Every generation must answer it, not according to tradition, family heritage, or popular opinion, but according to the testimony of God's Word. The answer cannot be borrowed from someone else, nor can it be inherited through religious affiliation. It is a question that requires a personal response.

Before anyone else spoke, Simon Peter answered with confidence. Having walked with Jesus, listened to His teaching, witnessed His miracles, and experienced His compassion, Peter declared what he had come to believe.

"And Simon Peter answered and said, Thou art the Christ, the Son of the living God."
Matthew 16:16

Peter's confession was far more than a statement of admiration or respect. He was not simply acknowledging that Jesus was an extraordinary man. He declared that Jesus was the Christ, the promised Messiah whom Israel had anticipated for generations. From the earliest pages of the Old Testament, God had promised that a Redeemer would come to deliver His people. Throughout the Law, the Prophets, and the Psalms, that promise was repeated in many different ways until it found its fulfillment in Jesus Christ.

When Peter called Jesus "the Christ," he recognized that God's promises were no longer something to anticipate. They were standing before him. The One whom Abraham looked toward in faith, whom Moses foreshadowed through the Passover, whom David celebrated in the Psalms, and whom Isaiah described as the suffering Servant had come exactly as God had promised.

This confession also reminds us that Christianity is not built upon human philosophy or moral instruction. It is founded upon the person of Jesus Christ. His teachings are authoritative because of who He is. His miracles testify to His authority because of who He is. His sacrifice on the cross has power to save because of who He is. Every truth of the gospel rests upon His identity.

For this reason, it is impossible to separate the Savior from the salvation He offers. Many people desire forgiveness without surrender, eternal life without repentance, or the blessings of God without knowing the One who gives them. The Scriptures never present salvation as merely accepting a set of beliefs or adopting a religious lifestyle. Salvation begins with a relationship with Jesus Christ, the One whom the Father sent into the world to redeem mankind.

Before we ask what Jesus can do for us, we must first understand who He is. Every chapter that follows in this book will continue building upon this truth, because every promise of the gospel, every invitation to salvation, and every call to discipleship finds its foundation in the identity of Jesus Christ.

Jesus did not ask His disciples that question because He lacked information. He already knew what people believed about Him. Instead, He asked the question because He wanted His followers to examine their own hearts. It is one thing to know what others believe about Jesus, but it is something entirely different to know Him personally through the truth of God's Word. Faith has never been built upon popular opinion. It has always rested upon God's revelation.

That same question continues to stand before every person today. It is not enough to know about Jesus through history books, family traditions, or the experiences of others. Every individual must come to the place where they personally answer the question that Jesus asked His disciples. Who is Jesus Christ? Is He merely a respected figure from history, or is He truly the Savior whom God has revealed in the Scriptures?

The Bible leaves no uncertainty regarding that answer. Jesus Christ is the promised Messiah, the Lamb of God who takes away the sin of the world, the Good Shepherd who lays down His life for His sheep, and the risen Lord who conquered death through His resurrection. He alone has the authority to forgive sins, reconcile humanity to God, and offer the gift of eternal life. There is no substitute for Him, no alternative path to salvation, and no other name through whom mankind can be saved.

As we continue through this book, we will examine many important subjects, including sin, repentance, faith, grace, forgiveness, baptism, discipleship, and the hope of eternal life. Each of these topics deserves careful attention, but none of them can be fully understood apart from Jesus Christ. He is not simply one subject among many. He is the center of the gospel message and the foundation upon which every other truth is established.

For that reason, this chapter is not intended to answer every question about the Savior. Rather, it serves as the beginning of a journey through the Scriptures. Together we will examine what God has revealed about His Son, allowing His Word to shape our understanding instead of relying upon personal opinions or religious traditions. As we do, we will discover that the gospel is not merely a message to be studied but the power of God that transforms lives through Jesus Christ.

"Neither is there salvation in any other: for there is none other name under heaven given among men, whereby we must be saved."
Acts 4:12

The Foundation Has Been Laid

The Journey Continues

Before we can understand what Jesus Christ accomplished, we must first understand who He is. With that foundation established, we are now ready to examine why every person needs a Savior and how God's plan of redemption unfolds throughout the Scriptures.

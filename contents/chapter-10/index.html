Who Do You Call Savior?

Chapter Ten

The Savior Still Calls Today

The Savior Still Calls Today

From the beginning of His earthly ministry, Jesus Christ extended an invitation unlike any the world had ever heard. He did not come searching for the powerful, the influential, or those who believed they had no need of God. He walked among ordinary people whose lives had been marked by failure, disappointment, sorrow, and sin. Fishermen left their nets to follow Him. Tax collectors abandoned their former lives. The sick found healing, the broken found hope, and sinners discovered forgiveness. Every invitation He gave was rooted in His grace rather than human merit.

The invitation of Jesus Christ has never changed. His grace, mercy, and salvation are still offered today.

Throughout this book we have discovered who Jesus Christ is, why every person needs a Savior, how His blood provides forgiveness, and why salvation is found in Him alone. Every chapter has pointed toward one glorious truth. Jesus Christ continues to call people to Himself.

Time has not weakened His invitation. Kingdoms have risen and fallen. Generations have passed away. Cultures have changed, and countless philosophies have promised hope apart from God. Yet through every age the voice of the Good Shepherd continues to call those who are weary, burdened, and searching for truth. His invitation is just as full of mercy today as it was when He first walked the roads of Galilee.

"Come unto me, all ye that labour and are heavy laden, and I will give you rest."
Matthew 11:28

These words reveal the very heart of Jesus Christ. He does not invite people because they have already overcome every failure or solved every problem. He invites those who recognize their need. The weary find rest in Him. The guilty find forgiveness. The hopeless discover lasting hope. Everything the sinner truly needs is found in Jesus Christ.

No other invitation carries such eternal significance. Christ does not simply promise temporary relief from life's burdens. He offers reconciliation with God, forgiveness of sin, peace for troubled hearts, and the gift of everlasting life. His invitation reaches beyond this present life and into eternity itself.

Throughout the Gospels we see this invitation extended again and again. Jesus welcomed those whom society often overlooked or rejected. He did not measure people by their failures, their reputation, or their past. He saw men and women created in the image of God who desperately needed His mercy. Wherever He traveled, lives were transformed because people encountered not only His power but also His compassion.

Fishermen became disciples. Tax collectors became followers. The blind received sight, the lame walked, and those oppressed by sin found freedom. Yet the greatest miracle Jesus performed was not restoring broken bodies. It was restoring broken souls. He came to forgive sin and reconcile people to God.

"For the Son of man is come to seek and to save that which was lost."
Luke 19:10

This mission has never changed. Jesus Christ did not come to condemn those who recognized their need for Him. He came to seek those who were lost and to accomplish the salvation that only He could provide. His mercy reached beyond every social barrier, every personal failure, and every religious tradition. Everyone who came to Him in humble faith discovered the same wonderful truth. The Savior was ready to receive them.

Perhaps you wonder whether His invitation truly includes you. Regret may remind you of past failures that cannot be undone. Shame may whisper that you have wandered too far from God. You may believe your mistakes have placed you beyond His forgiveness. Many people quietly carry these burdens, convinced that grace is available for everyone except themselves.

The Scriptures tell a far different story. Again and again Jesus welcomed those who believed their lives were beyond hope. Zacchaeus discovered that salvation had come to his house. The sinful woman found mercy instead of condemnation. The thief on the cross received the promise of paradise in the final hours of his life. Their stories remind us that no sinner is beyond the reach of God's grace.

"Him that cometh to me I will in no wise cast out."
John 6:37

What a remarkable promise. Jesus does not say that He welcomes only those who have cleaned up their lives or earned His acceptance. He promises that everyone who comes to Him will be received. His invitation is not limited by age, nationality, education, background, or past mistakes. The only requirement is to come in faith.

That promise fills the gospel with hope. No life is too broken. No sin is too great. No heart is too far away. The same Savior who welcomed weary souls during His earthly ministry continues to receive all who come to Him today.

The invitation of Jesus Christ is not merely a message from the past. It is the living call of the gospel that continues to reach hearts today. Every generation has faced the same decision, and every individual must answer the same question. Will I come to the Savior, or will I walk away from His invitation? God does not force anyone to believe, but He lovingly calls every person to receive the salvation He has provided through His Son.

The urgency of this invitation should never be overlooked. Life is uncertain, and tomorrow is never guaranteed. Many people convince themselves that they will seek God someday, after life becomes less busy or after they have accomplished their own plans. Yet the Scriptures continually remind us that salvation is not an opportunity to postpone. It is an invitation to receive while His call is being heard.

"(For he saith, I have heard thee in a time accepted, and in the day of salvation have I succoured thee: behold, now is the accepted time; behold, now is the day of salvation.)"
2 Corinthians 6:2

God's invitation is both gracious and urgent. His patience reveals His mercy, giving people the opportunity to repent and believe the gospel. Yet His patience should never be mistaken for permission to delay. Every new day is another expression of God's kindness, another reminder that the door of grace remains open through Jesus Christ.

Responding to Christ is far more than agreeing with certain facts about Him. It is placing our complete trust in Him as Lord and Savior. It is turning away from confidence in ourselves and resting entirely upon His finished work on the cross. The gospel does not simply invite us to adopt a new religion. It invites us into a living relationship with the risen Savior.

This invitation reaches every person without exception. Whether someone has spent years searching for God or has only recently begun asking spiritual questions, the message remains the same. The Savior still calls. His mercy is still available. His grace is still sufficient. His promises remain just as certain today as they were when He first spoke them.

"For whosoever shall call upon the name of the Lord shall be saved."
Romans 10:13

The word whosoever shines with extraordinary hope. It reminds us that the gospel reaches across every nation, every generation, every language, and every culture. No one is rejected because of a sinful past, and no one is accepted because of personal goodness. Every person comes to God in exactly the same way, through Jesus Christ alone.

This has always been the heart of the gospel. The Savior who welcomed the weary, forgave sinners, healed the broken, and restored the hopeless continues to extend His invitation today. His grace has not diminished. His compassion has not faded. His power to save has not changed. Jesus Christ is still calling people to Himself.

The invitation of Jesus Christ deserves a response. Throughout the Gospels, every person who encountered Him was faced with a decision. Some believed and gladly followed Him. Others listened for a time but eventually walked away. Still others rejected Him altogether. Although the responses were different, His invitation remained the same. Jesus lovingly called people to come to Him.

That same invitation continues today. Every reader of this book must answer the same question. What will I do with Jesus Christ? No one else can answer that question for us. Family members cannot believe on our behalf. Friends cannot receive salvation for us. Church membership, religious traditions, and good intentions can never take the place of a personal response to the Savior.

"Seek ye the LORD while he may be found, call ye upon him while he is near: Let the wicked forsake his way, and the unrighteous man his thoughts: and let him return unto the LORD, and he will have mercy upon him; and to our God, for he will abundantly pardon."
Isaiah 55:6–7

What a wonderful promise. God does not reluctantly receive those who come to Him. He welcomes them with mercy. He does not offer partial forgiveness or temporary acceptance. He abundantly pardons those who turn to Him in sincere faith. The grace of God is greater than every sin, stronger than every failure, and sufficient for every person who comes to Jesus Christ.

Some hesitate because they believe they must first change themselves before coming to Christ. The gospel teaches the opposite. We do not come because we have become worthy. We come because we know we are unable to save ourselves. Jesus Christ receives those who trust in Him, and through His grace He begins the lifelong work of transforming them into His likeness.

Others hesitate because they fear they have waited too long. They wonder whether years of sin, indifference, or rebellion have placed them beyond God's forgiveness. The Scriptures answer that fear with hope. As long as Christ's invitation is being proclaimed, His mercy is available. The Savior still receives those who come to Him with humble and believing hearts.

If you already belong to Jesus Christ, never lose your wonder at His invitation. The gospel is not only the message that brought you to salvation. It is the message that sustains you every day. We continue coming to Christ for strength, wisdom, peace, forgiveness, grace, and hope because He remains everything we need.

"Jesus Christ the same yesterday, and to day, and for ever."
Hebrews 13:8

Because Jesus Christ never changes, His invitation never changes. His love remains steadfast. His mercy remains abundant. His promises remain certain. The same Savior who welcomed weary souls two thousand years ago continues to welcome all who come to Him today. His arms remain open, His grace remains sufficient, and His salvation remains available to everyone who believes.

As we conclude this final chapter, one truth rises above every other. The invitation of Jesus Christ has never changed because the heart of Jesus Christ has never changed. From the beginning of His earthly ministry until this very moment, He continues calling people to Himself with grace, mercy, and truth. The gospel is not merely a message to be admired. It is an invitation to be received.

The Savior still calls. The question is whether you will answer.

Every chapter of this book has pointed to the same unchanging truth. Jesus Christ is the only Savior. Every promise of forgiveness, every message of hope, and every invitation to eternal life is found in Him alone. His call continues to go out to every person who is willing to come by faith.

Perhaps you have followed Jesus Christ for many years. If so, let this chapter remind you of the grace that first drew you to Him. Never lose your wonder at the mercy that rescued your soul. Continue walking faithfully with the Savior who has promised never to leave you nor forsake you. Let your life reflect His love and faithfully point others to Him.

Perhaps you have read these pages with questions still lingering in your heart. You may wonder whether Jesus would truly receive someone like you. You may feel the weight of past sins, broken relationships, or years spent living apart from God. Hear His invitation once more. He has not turned you away. His grace is still sufficient. His mercy is still available. His invitation still stands.

"And the Spirit and the bride say, Come. And let him that heareth say, Come. And let him that is athirst come. And whosoever will, let him take the water of life freely."
Revelation 22:17

The Bible closes with an invitation rather than a farewell. From Genesis to Revelation, God's desire has been to redeem people through Jesus Christ. Today that invitation is still extended. Today His grace is still offered. Today His mercy is still available. There has never been a greater invitation because there has never been a greater Savior.

"Neither is there salvation in any other: for there is none other name under heaven given among men, whereby we must be saved."
Acts 4:12

As you turn to the Epilogue, do not simply remember the chapters you have read. Remember the Savior they proclaim. Every page of this book has been written with one purpose: to direct your eyes to Jesus Christ. He alone forgives sin. He alone gives eternal life. He alone reconciles us to God. He alone is worthy of our faith, our worship, and our lives.

One Final Invitation

The Journey Continues

The invitation of Jesus Christ has never changed. His grace has not diminished, His mercy has not ended, and His salvation is still offered to every person who comes to Him in faith. As you continue to the Epilogue, return one final time to the question that has echoed throughout this entire book. Who do you call Savior?

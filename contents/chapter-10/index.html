Who Do You Call Savior?

Chapter Ten

The Savior Still Calls Today

The Savior Still Calls Today

From the very beginning of His earthly ministry, Jesus Christ extended an invitation unlike any the world had ever heard. He did not call only the educated, the wealthy, the influential, or the religious. He called ordinary men and women. He invited fishermen, tax collectors, sinners, the brokenhearted, and those who recognized their desperate need for God. His invitation was not based upon human worthiness but upon His perfect grace.

The invitation of Jesus Christ has never changed. His grace, mercy, and salvation are still offered today.

As we have journeyed through this book, we have discovered who Jesus is, why every person needs a Savior, how forgiveness is found through His blood, what salvation truly means, and how believers can rest in the assurance of God's promises. Every one of these truths leads to the same conclusion. Jesus Christ is still calling people to Himself.

The passing of time has not weakened His invitation. Generations have come and gone. Nations have risen and fallen. Cultures have changed, and countless voices have offered competing answers to life's greatest questions. Yet through every generation, the voice of the Good Shepherd continues to call those who are willing to hear Him. His invitation remains as full of grace today as it was when He first spoke beside the Sea of Galilee.

"Come unto me, all ye that labour and are heavy laden, and I will give you rest."
Matthew 11:28

These words reveal the heart of our Savior. He does not invite people because they have already solved every problem or overcome every failure. He calls those who are weary, burdened, and searching for hope. His invitation is extended to people who recognize that they cannot save themselves and who are willing to place their trust completely in Him.

No other invitation carries such eternal significance. Jesus does not merely offer temporary relief from life's difficulties. He offers forgiveness for sin, reconciliation with God, peace for troubled hearts, and the promise of everlasting life. Everything the sinner truly needs is found in Him.

Throughout the Gospels we repeatedly see Jesus welcoming those whom others had rejected. Society often measured people by their failures, their reputations, or their past. Jesus saw something different. He saw men and women created in the image of God who desperately needed the mercy that only He could provide. Wherever He traveled, His compassion reached those who believed they had been forgotten.

Tax collectors found acceptance in His presence. Fishermen were called to become disciples. Those bound by sickness experienced healing. The blind received sight, the lame walked again, and broken lives were restored through His power. Yet the greatest miracle was not physical healing. It was the forgiveness of sins and the gift of new life that Jesus came to offer.

"For the Son of man is come to seek and to save that which was lost."
Luke 19:10

That mission has never changed. Jesus did not come to condemn those who recognized their need for Him. He came to seek those who were lost and to provide the salvation that only He could accomplish. His love reached beyond social barriers, personal failures, and religious traditions. Every person who came to Him in faith discovered the same truth. The Savior was willing to receive them.

Perhaps you sometimes wonder whether God's invitation truly includes you. You may look back over your life and remember failures you wish you could erase. Regret may whisper that you have wandered too far or sinned too greatly to ever experience God's mercy. Many people carry these burdens in silence, believing they are somehow beyond the reach of His grace.

The Scriptures tell a very different story. Again and again we find Jesus extending mercy to those who believed their lives were beyond hope. The woman caught in adultery found forgiveness instead of condemnation. Zacchaeus discovered that salvation had come to his house. The thief on the cross received the promise of paradise in the final moments of his life. These accounts remind us that the grace of God is greater than the failures of humanity.

"Him that cometh to me I will in no wise cast out."
John 6:37

What an incredible promise. Jesus does not say that He will receive only those with perfect pasts or flawless lives. He promises that the one who comes to Him will never be turned away. His invitation is not limited by age, background, nationality, education, or previous mistakes. The only requirement is that we come to Him in faith.

This truth fills the gospel with hope. No one is beyond the reach of God's grace. No life is too broken for the Savior to restore. No sinner is so far away that Christ cannot forgive, redeem, and make all things new. The same Lord who called people to Himself during His earthly ministry continues to extend that gracious invitation today.

The invitation of Jesus Christ is not merely a message from the past. It is the living call of the gospel that continues to reach hearts today. Every generation has faced the same decision. Every individual must answer the same question. Will I respond to the Savior, or will I turn away from His invitation? God does not force anyone to come to Him, but He lovingly calls every person to receive the salvation He has provided through His Son.

The urgency of this invitation should never be overlooked. Life is uncertain, and tomorrow is never guaranteed. Many people convince themselves that they will seek God someday, after life becomes less busy or after they have accomplished their own plans. Yet the Bible continually reminds us that salvation is an invitation to be received today, not postponed until a more convenient time.

"(For he saith, I have heard thee in a time accepted, and in the day of salvation have I succoured thee: behold, now is the accepted time; behold, now is the day of salvation.)"
2 Corinthians 6:2

God's invitation is both gracious and urgent. His patience is a demonstration of His mercy, giving people opportunity to repent and believe the gospel. Yet His patience should never be mistaken for permission to delay. Every passing day is another reminder of the kindness of God, who continues calling people to Himself while the door of grace remains open.

Responding to Christ is not simply agreeing with certain facts about Him. It is placing our trust completely in Him as Lord and Savior. It is turning away from confidence in ourselves and resting entirely in His finished work upon the cross. The gospel invites us into a relationship with the living Christ, not merely into a religious tradition or system of beliefs.

This invitation reaches every person regardless of background. Whether someone has spent years pursuing God or has only recently begun asking spiritual questions, the message remains the same. The Savior still calls. His mercy is still available. His grace is still sufficient. His promises remain as certain today as they were when He first spoke them.

"For whosoever shall call upon the name of the Lord shall be saved."
Romans 10:13

The word whosoever reminds us that the gospel is wonderfully inclusive. God's invitation extends across every nation, every generation, every language, and every circumstance. No one is excluded because of a sinful past, and no one is accepted because of personal merit. Every person comes to God in exactly the same way, through faith in Jesus Christ alone.

This has been the heartbeat of the gospel from the beginning. The Savior who welcomed the weary, forgave sinners, healed the broken, and gave hope to the hopeless continues to extend His hands of mercy today. His invitation has not expired. His grace has not diminished. His power to save has not changed.

The invitation of Jesus Christ deserves a response. Throughout the Gospels, every person who encountered Him was faced with a decision. Some believed and followed Him with joyful faith. Others admired His teaching but refused to surrender their lives. Still others rejected Him altogether. Although the responses were different, the invitation remained the same. Jesus called each person to come to Him.

That same invitation continues today. Every reader of this book must eventually answer the same question. What will I do with Jesus Christ? No one else can answer that question for us. Family members cannot believe on our behalf. Friends cannot receive salvation for us. Church attendance, religious traditions, and good intentions cannot take the place of a personal response to the Savior.

"Seek ye the LORD while he may be found, call ye upon him while he is near: Let the wicked forsake his way, and the unrighteous man his thoughts: and let him return unto the LORD, and he will have mercy upon him; and to our God, for he will abundantly pardon."
Isaiah 55:6–7

What a remarkable promise. God does not merely tolerate those who come to Him. He welcomes them with mercy. He does not offer partial forgiveness or reluctant acceptance. He abundantly pardons those who turn to Him in faith. The grace of God is greater than every sin, stronger than every failure, and sufficient for every person who comes to Jesus Christ.

Some people hesitate because they believe they must first change themselves before coming to Christ. The gospel teaches the opposite. We do not come because we have already become worthy. We come because we recognize that we are unable to save ourselves. Jesus Christ receives those who place their trust in Him, and through His grace He begins the lifelong work of transforming them into His likeness.

Others hesitate because they fear they have waited too long. They wonder whether years of rebellion or indifference have placed them beyond God's forgiveness. The pages of Scripture continually answer that fear with hope. As long as God's invitation is extended, His mercy remains available. The Savior still receives those who come to Him with humble and believing hearts.

For those who have already trusted in Jesus Christ, this invitation should never lose its wonder. The gospel is not only the message that brought us to salvation. It is the message that sustains us every day. We continue coming to Christ for strength, wisdom, peace, forgiveness, and grace because He remains everything we need. The Savior who first called us continues to walk faithfully with us throughout our lives.

"Jesus Christ the same yesterday, and to day, and for ever."
Hebrews 13:8

Because Jesus never changes, His invitation never changes. His love remains steadfast. His mercy remains abundant. His promises remain certain. The same Savior who welcomed weary souls two thousand years ago continues to welcome all who come to Him today. His arms remain open, His grace remains sufficient, and His salvation remains available to everyone who believes.

As we conclude this chapter, one truth stands above every other. The invitation of Jesus Christ has never changed because the heart of Jesus Christ has never changed. From the beginning of His ministry until this very moment, He continues calling people to Himself with grace, mercy, and truth. The gospel is not merely a message to be admired. It is an invitation to be received.

The Savior still calls. The question is whether we will answer.

Throughout this book we have explored the identity of Jesus Christ, the reality of sin, the necessity of the cross, the power of His blood, the gift of salvation, the assurance believers possess, and the confidence that rests in Him alone. Every chapter has pointed toward this wonderful conclusion. Jesus Christ is the only Savior, and His invitation is extended to every person without exception.

Perhaps you have followed Jesus for many years. If so, let this chapter remind you of the grace that first brought you to Him. Never lose your wonder at the mercy that rescued your soul. Continue walking faithfully with the Savior who has promised never to leave nor forsake His people.

Perhaps you are still searching. You have questions, struggles, or doubts that have kept you from fully trusting Christ. Know this above all else. The invitation of the gospel remains open. Jesus Christ has not turned away from you. His arms are still open. His mercy is still available. His promise still stands for every person who comes to Him in faith.

"And the Spirit and the bride say, Come. And let him that heareth say, Come. And let him that is athirst come. And whosoever will, let him take the water of life freely."
Revelation 22:17

The Bible closes with an invitation rather than a farewell. God continues calling people to Himself. His desire is not that anyone should perish but that all would come to the knowledge of the truth. The same voice that called weary fishermen beside the Sea of Galilee still calls weary hearts today. His invitation reaches across every nation, every generation, and every circumstance.

One day the opportunity to respond will come to an end, but today His grace is still extended. Today His mercy is still available. Today His salvation is still offered through Jesus Christ. There has never been a greater invitation, and there will never be another Savior.

"Neither is there salvation in any other: for there is none other name under heaven given among men, whereby we must be saved."
Acts 4:12

May you leave this chapter with your eyes fixed upon Jesus Christ. Whether you are coming to Him for the first time or continuing to follow Him after many years, remember that He remains faithful. His promises are true. His grace is sufficient. His mercy is everlasting. The Savior still calls today, and all who come to Him will find forgiveness, peace, and everlasting life.

The Invitation Remains

Come to the Savior

Jesus Christ continues to call every heart to Himself. His grace has not diminished, His mercy has not ended, and His invitation has not changed. Come to Him in faith, trust in His finished work, and discover the eternal hope that is found in the Savior alone.

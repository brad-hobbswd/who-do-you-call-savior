Who Do You Call Savior?

Chapter Eight

How Can I Know I Am Saved?

How Can I Know I Am Saved?

One of the most common questions sincere believers ask is, "How can I know that I am truly saved?" This question often arises after a person has placed his or her faith in Jesus Christ. While the joy of salvation is real, there are times when doubts, fears, or difficult circumstances cause believers to question what God has already accomplished. The Scriptures recognize these struggles and lovingly direct us away from uncertainty and toward the confidence that is found in Jesus Christ.

Our assurance is not rooted in our feelings. It is rooted in the unchanging promises of God.

Human emotions are constantly changing. There are days when we feel close to God and days when we feel distant. There are moments when our faith seems strong and other times when we wrestle with questions and discouragement. If our confidence depended upon our emotions, we would never possess lasting peace because our feelings often change with our circumstances.

God's promises, however, never change. His character is perfect, His Word is true, and His faithfulness is constant. The believer's assurance is not built upon fluctuating emotions but upon the finished work of Jesus Christ and the promises that God has given in the Scriptures.

"These things have I written unto you that believe on the name of the Son of God; that ye may know that ye have eternal life."
1 John 5:13

Notice John's purpose for writing these words. He did not say believers should merely hope they have eternal life or wonder whether they belong to Christ. He declared that they may know. Biblical assurance is not based upon wishful thinking. It rests upon the certainty of God's promises. Because God cannot lie, those who have trusted in Jesus Christ may have confidence in the salvation He has provided.

This confidence does not come from believing that we have lived perfectly or earned God's acceptance. It comes from trusting the One who lived perfectly on our behalf. Jesus Christ accomplished everything necessary for our salvation through His death, burial, and resurrection. Our confidence rests in Him, not in ourselves.

"My sheep hear my voice, and I know them, and they follow me: And I give unto them eternal life; and they shall never perish, neither shall any man pluck them out of my hand."
John 10:27–28

What a wonderful promise. Jesus describes His followers as sheep who know His voice and follow Him. More importantly, He declares that eternal life is His gift and that those who belong to Him are securely held in His care. Our assurance rests not in our ability to hold on to Christ but in His power to faithfully keep those who belong to Him.

While God's promises provide the foundation of our assurance, He has also given believers another source of encouragement. The Holy Spirit, who comes to dwell within every person who has been born again, continually testifies that we belong to God. This inward witness does not replace the authority of Scripture, nor does it create new revelation. Rather, the Holy Spirit confirms the truth of God's Word within the heart of the believer and produces the confidence that comes from knowing Jesus Christ personally.

"The Spirit itself beareth witness with our spirit, that we are the children of God."
Romans 8:16

This witness of the Holy Spirit is one of the gracious gifts God gives to His children. As believers grow in their relationship with Christ, they become increasingly aware of His presence, His guidance, His conviction, and His peace. The Spirit reminds us of God's promises, strengthens us during seasons of temptation, comforts us in times of sorrow, and continually points our hearts back to Jesus Christ.

This does not mean believers will never experience moments of doubt. Throughout Scripture, faithful men and women occasionally struggled with fear, discouragement, and uncertainty. Even during those difficult seasons, God's faithfulness never changed. His promises remained true whether His people fully understood their circumstances or not.

For that reason, assurance should never be measured by our emotions alone. There are days when joy fills our hearts, and there are days when burdens weigh heavily upon us. There are moments when prayer seems effortless, and there are seasons when it requires perseverance. None of these changing experiences alter the promises of God. His truth remains constant because His character never changes.

When doubts arise, believers should not search endlessly within themselves for reassurance. Instead, they should return to the Word of God. The Scriptures continually direct our attention away from our weaknesses and toward the perfect sufficiency of Jesus Christ. Our confidence grows as we meditate upon His promises rather than our feelings.

"Let us hold fast the profession of our faith without wavering; (for he is faithful that promised;)"
Hebrews 10:23

Notice that the writer points us to God's faithfulness rather than our own. We are encouraged to hold firmly to our confession because the One who made the promise is completely trustworthy. Our assurance rests upon His faithfulness, not upon our ability to maintain perfect confidence every moment of every day.

As believers continue walking with Christ, they also begin to see the evidence of His work within their lives. Although no Christian reaches perfection in this life, genuine salvation produces genuine transformation. New desires begin to replace old ones. Love for God's Word grows stronger. Sin brings conviction rather than comfort. Obedience becomes a joyful response to God's grace rather than a burdensome obligation. These changes do not save us, but they do testify that the Holy Spirit is actively at work within us.

The Christian's assurance is therefore strengthened from several directions. God's Word gives unchanging promises. The Holy Spirit bears witness within the believer. A transformed life provides growing evidence of God's work. Together these blessings continually direct our confidence away from ourselves and back to the Savior who has promised to complete the work He began.

One of the clearest evidences of genuine salvation is a transformed life. The Bible never teaches that believers become perfect the moment they are saved, but it does teach that those who have truly trusted Jesus Christ will begin to experience the transforming work of the Holy Spirit. Salvation changes not only a person's eternal destination but also the direction of his or her daily life.

This transformation is not produced by human determination alone. It is the work of God within the believer. As Christians grow in their relationship with Jesus Christ, their thoughts, desires, attitudes, and actions are gradually shaped according to His will. The life that once pursued sin begins to pursue righteousness. Although the journey of spiritual growth continues throughout life, the direction has forever changed.

"Therefore if any man be in Christ, he is a new creature: old things are passed away; behold, all things are become new."
2 Corinthians 5:17

This verse does not suggest that every weakness disappears immediately after salvation. Rather, it declares that God has begun something entirely new. The believer now possesses a new identity in Jesus Christ. Old priorities begin giving way to new ones. Love for God grows stronger, and the desire to please Him becomes increasingly evident. These changes are not the cause of salvation. They are the evidence that salvation has already taken place.

Jesus taught that the condition of a person's heart is eventually revealed through the fruit of his or her life. Just as a healthy tree naturally produces good fruit, a life transformed by God's grace will gradually display the character of Christ. This fruit develops over time as believers continue abiding in Him and walking in the power of the Holy Spirit.

"Wherefore by their fruits ye shall know them."
Matthew 7:20

The fruit Jesus described is not limited to outward religious activity. It includes the inward qualities that the Holy Spirit produces within every growing believer. Love begins replacing bitterness. Joy remains even during difficult seasons. Peace steadies the heart in uncertain circumstances. Patience grows where frustration once ruled. Kindness, goodness, faithfulness, gentleness, and self control increasingly become evident because Christ is at work within His people.

Believers also discover a growing love for God's Word. The Scriptures become more than a book to read occasionally. They become daily nourishment for the soul. The desire to know God, understand His truth, and obey His commandments steadily increases as the Holy Spirit opens our hearts to His Word.

Likewise, our relationship with sin begins to change. Before coming to Christ, sin may have been accepted, defended, or ignored. After salvation, the Holy Spirit brings conviction when we disobey God. Rather than becoming comfortable with sin, believers grieve over it, confess it, and seek the Lord's forgiveness. This ongoing conviction is not evidence that God has abandoned His children. It is evidence that He is faithfully working within them.

These evidences should encourage believers rather than discourage them. None of us grows at exactly the same pace, and none of us reaches perfection in this life. Spiritual growth is a lifelong process. What matters is not flawless performance but a heart that continually desires to follow Jesus Christ. The God who began the work of salvation also faithfully continues the work of transformation.

"Being confident of this very thing, that he which hath begun a good work in you will perform it until the day of Jesus Christ."
Philippians 1:6

This promise reminds us that our assurance rests in God's faithfulness. The same Savior who called us by His grace continues to shape us by His grace. As we look back over our lives, we begin to see His patient work of transformation. That growing evidence strengthens our confidence, not because we trust in ourselves, but because we trust the One who never fails to complete what He has begun.

As encouraging as these evidences are, our ultimate assurance does not rest upon what we observe in ourselves. If our confidence depended entirely upon our own faithfulness, we would always find reasons to doubt. Every believer experiences moments of weakness, seasons of spiritual dryness, and times when faith must persevere through difficulty. If salvation depended upon our ability to hold firmly to God, none of us could stand with confidence. Our assurance rests upon the far greater truth that God faithfully holds those who belong to Him.

Throughout the Scriptures, God reveals Himself as a faithful Keeper of His people. He does not save a person only to abandon them when trials come. The One who begins the work of salvation also promises to sustain His children by His grace. Our security is not found in the strength of our grip upon Christ but in the strength of His grip upon us.

"And I give unto them eternal life; and they shall never perish, neither shall any man pluck them out of my hand."
John 10:28

These words of Jesus provide profound comfort for every believer. Eternal life is His gift, not our achievement. The Good Shepherd knows His sheep, calls them by name, and faithfully watches over them. No external force has the power to separate His people from His loving care. The believer's confidence rests not in personal ability but in the unwavering faithfulness of the Savior.

This assurance should never lead us to spiritual carelessness. God's grace does not encourage indifference toward holiness. Instead, it inspires gratitude, reverence, and faithful obedience. Those who truly understand the love of Christ desire to honor Him, not because they are trying to earn His acceptance, but because they have already received His mercy.

There may be times when believers stumble into sin. During those moments, the enemy often seeks to fill the heart with discouragement, whispering that failure has placed us beyond God's forgiveness. The Scriptures answer those accusations by directing us back to Jesus Christ. Our hope has never rested upon flawless performance. It has always rested upon the perfect righteousness of our Savior.

"My little children, these things write I unto you, that ye sin not. And if any man sin, we have an advocate with the Father, Jesus Christ the righteous."
1 John 2:1

John does not encourage believers to continue in sin. Rather, he reminds us that when we fail, we are not left without hope. Jesus Christ continues to intercede on behalf of those who belong to Him. His sacrifice remains sufficient, His grace remains available, and His mercy continues to meet every repentant heart.

Because our assurance rests in Christ, we can face each day with confidence rather than fear. We are free to grow in faith without living under the constant anxiety of wondering whether God has abandoned us. His promises remain true in seasons of joy and in seasons of trial. His love is steadfast, His grace is sufficient, and His purposes for His children will never fail.

For this reason, believers are called to fix their eyes continually upon Jesus Christ. The more we know Him through His Word, the more our confidence grows. Our assurance becomes stronger not because we become more confident in ourselves, but because we become more confident in the Savior who has promised never to leave nor forsake His people.

"Looking unto Jesus the author and finisher of our faith."
Hebrews 12:2

Every promise of assurance ultimately leads us back to Him. Jesus Christ is the foundation of our salvation, the source of our confidence, and the One who faithfully completes the work He has begun. As we continue walking with Him, we may rest in the certainty that our hope is secure because it rests in the unchanging character of our Savior.

As we conclude this chapter, we return to the question that has guided our study from the beginning.

How can I know that I am saved?

The answer is not found by examining our emotions or comparing ourselves with other people. It is found by looking to Jesus Christ and believing the promises God has given in His Word. Our confidence rests upon the finished work of the cross, the empty tomb, and the unchanging faithfulness of the Savior. God has not left His children to wonder whether they belong to Him. He has given His promises, His Spirit, and His Word so that believers may walk with confidence rather than uncertainty.

There will undoubtedly be seasons when questions arise. Trials, temptations, suffering, and personal failures may cause us to examine our hearts. During those moments, we must resist the temptation to build our assurance upon changing circumstances. Instead, we must return again and again to the truth of the gospel. Jesus Christ remains the same yesterday, today, and forever. His promises have not changed because our circumstances have changed.

"Jesus Christ the same yesterday, and to day, and for ever."
Hebrews 13:8

Believers should never confuse assurance with pride. Biblical assurance does not lead us to boast in ourselves. It leads us to worship the Savior. Every confidence we possess is rooted in His grace. Every hope we cherish rests upon His mercy. Every promise we embrace is secured through His faithfulness. The more we understand the greatness of Jesus Christ, the greater our assurance becomes.

Likewise, assurance should produce gratitude rather than complacency. Those who know they have been redeemed desire to honor the One who redeemed them. Love for Christ becomes the motivation for obedience. Faithfulness becomes the joyful response to His grace. The Christian life is not lived in an effort to earn God's acceptance but because we have already received His acceptance through Jesus Christ.

When doubts arise, remember where your confidence belongs. Look to the cross where your sin was paid for. Look to the empty tomb where death was conquered. Look to the Word of God that never fails. Look to the Holy Spirit who faithfully leads believers into truth. Above all, look to Jesus Christ, the Savior who has promised to complete the work He has begun in every believer.

"Being confident of this very thing, that he which hath begun a good work in you will perform it until the day of Jesus Christ."
Philippians 1:6

This confidence is not wishful thinking. It is the settled assurance that comes from trusting a faithful God. The One who called us by His grace will continue to guide us by His grace until the day we stand before Him. Our salvation is secure because our Savior is faithful.

As we continue through this book, we will discover that assurance is not intended to bring our spiritual journey to an end. Instead, it gives us the confidence to continue growing in our relationship with Jesus Christ. Those who know they belong to Him are free to serve Him with joy, to trust Him in every circumstance, and to live each day with the hope of eternity before them.

Confidence in Christ

Rest in His Promises

Our assurance rests not in our own strength but in the unchanging promises of God. As we continue our journey, we will discover how a life anchored in Christ grows through obedience, faithfulness, and a daily dependence upon the Savior who never fails.

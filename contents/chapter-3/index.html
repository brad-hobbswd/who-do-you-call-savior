Who Do You Call Savior?

Chapter Three

The Blood That Speaks Better

The Blood That Speaks Better

In the previous chapter, we discovered that every person stands on equal ground before God. No one is righteous enough to save themselves, and no one is beyond the reach of His grace. Once we understand that every person needs a Savior, another question naturally follows.

Why Did Jesus Have to Shed His Blood?

From Genesis to Revelation, the answer never changes. Sin separates humanity from God, and only the sacrifice He provided could restore that relationship. The blood of Jesus Christ is not merely the story of His suffering. It is the foundation of our redemption.

Throughout Scripture, blood represents life. It reminds us that sin carries a cost and that forgiveness is never earned by human effort. Every sacrifice in the Old Testament pointed beyond itself to a greater sacrifice that God had already planned before the foundation of the world.

After Adam and Eve sinned, they attempted to cover their shame with garments they had made for themselves. Their efforts could not remove their guilt or restore fellowship with God. Instead, God provided a covering for them, revealing a truth that echoes throughout the Bible. Salvation begins with God's provision, not ours.

As history unfolds, that message becomes clearer. Abel offered a sacrifice by faith. Noah built an altar after the flood. Abraham prepared to offer Isaac before God provided a ram in his place. During the Passover, the blood of the lamb protected Israel from judgment. Under the Law of Moses, sacrifices were continually offered upon the altar.

Each sacrifice declared the same promise.

Who Was Every Sacrifice Pointing To?

"Behold the Lamb of God, which taketh away the sin of the world."
John 1:29

When John the Baptist spoke these words, he introduced Jesus Christ in a way no one else ever had. Jesus was not simply another prophet, teacher, or miracle worker. He was the Lamb of God, the fulfillment of every sacrifice that had come before Him.

For centuries, lambs had been offered upon Israel's altars. Their blood reminded God's people that sin demanded judgment, yet those sacrifices could never permanently remove sin. They pointed to the day when God Himself would provide the perfect sacrifice.

Jesus Christ fulfilled that promise. His blood would not temporarily cover sin. It would completely satisfy God's justice and provide redemption for everyone who believes in Him.

Why Was Blood Required?

God is perfectly holy. Because of His holiness, sin cannot simply be ignored. Justice requires that sin be judged, and from the beginning of Scripture, God declared that the consequence of sin is death.

The sacrifices under the Law continually reminded God's people that forgiveness is costly. Every sacrifice testified that life had been given because of sin.

"For the life of the flesh is in the blood: and I have given it to you upon the altar to make an atonement for your souls: for it is the blood that maketh an atonement for the soul."
Leviticus 17:11

Blood represented life given in the place of another. Yet even these sacrifices could never permanently remove guilt. Year after year they were repeated, reminding Israel that a greater sacrifice was still to come.

"For it is not possible that the blood of bulls and of goats should take away sins."
Hebrews 10:4

Those sacrifices were shadows. Jesus Christ is the reality. He lived the sinless life that no one else could live and willingly offered Himself as the perfect sacrifice for sinners. Unlike the priests of the Old Covenant, He did not bring the blood of another. He offered His own.

Because Jesus is the spotless Lamb of God, His sacrifice never needs to be repeated. His blood accomplished forever what countless sacrifices could never achieve.

What Does His Blood Accomplish?

The blood of Jesus Christ does far more than remind us of His suffering. Through His blood, God provides redemption, forgiveness, cleansing, reconciliation, and the assurance of eternal life.

"In whom we have redemption through his blood, the forgiveness of sins, according to the riches of his grace."
Ephesians 1:7

Through His blood we are redeemed. The price has been paid. The penalty of sin has been satisfied. Forgiveness is no longer something we hope to earn. It is a gift God freely gives through Jesus Christ.

"But if we walk in the light, as he is in the light, we have fellowship one with another, and the blood of Jesus Christ his Son cleanseth us from all sin."
1 John 1:7

Notice the promise. His blood cleanses us from all sin. No life is too broken, no past too dark, and no failure too great for the cleansing power of Jesus Christ.

"But now in Christ Jesus ye who sometimes were far off are made nigh by the blood of Christ."
Ephesians 2:13

Through His blood, those who were separated from God are brought near. Salvation is more than the forgiveness of sins. It is the restoration of a relationship with our Creator. The cross does not simply remove our guilt. It welcomes us into the presence of God through Jesus Christ.

Why Does His Blood Speak Better?

The work accomplished through the blood of Jesus Christ did not end at Calvary. The writer of Hebrews reminds us that His blood still speaks today. Unlike the sacrifices offered under the Old Covenant, His sacrifice never loses its power or its significance. It remains the complete and final provision for our salvation.

"And to Jesus the mediator of the new covenant, and to the blood of sprinkling, that speaketh better things than that of Abel."
Hebrews 12:24

To understand these words, we must return to the first family recorded in Scripture. After Cain murdered his brother Abel, God confronted him with a sobering declaration.

"And he said, What hast thou done? the voice of thy brother's blood crieth unto me from the ground."
Genesis 4:10

Abel's blood cried out because an innocent life had been taken. It testified that sin deserved judgment and that justice must be satisfied.

The blood of Jesus Christ speaks a far better message.

Abel's blood cried for justice. Christ's blood proclaims mercy.

Abel's blood testified against the sinner. Christ's blood intercedes for the repentant. Abel's blood revealed humanity's guilt. Christ's blood reveals God's grace. Abel's blood pointed to death. Christ's blood offers everlasting life.

Because of the sacrifice of Jesus Christ, believers no longer approach God trusting in their own goodness. We come with confidence because the perfect sacrifice has already been offered on our behalf.

"Having therefore, brethren, boldness to enter into the holiest by the blood of Jesus."
Hebrews 10:19

Our confidence is not found in religious traditions, personal achievements, or good works. It rests entirely upon the finished work of Jesus Christ. Nothing can be added to His sacrifice, and nothing will ever replace it.

Whenever guilt reminds us of our failures, His blood reminds us of His grace. Whenever fear whispers that we are too far gone, His blood declares that forgiveness is available to everyone who comes to Him in faith.

From Genesis to Revelation, the Bible tells one story of redemption, and at the center of that story stands Jesus Christ. Every sacrifice anticipated Him. Every prophecy pointed toward Him. Every promise finds its fulfillment in Him. His blood still speaks today, proclaiming forgiveness instead of condemnation, hope instead of despair, life instead of death, and peace with God for all who believe.

Looking Ahead

Grace for Every Heart

The blood of Jesus Christ accomplished everything necessary for our salvation. The next chapter reveals that this grace is not reserved for a select few. It reaches every broken heart, every weary soul, and every person willing to come to the Savior in faith.
